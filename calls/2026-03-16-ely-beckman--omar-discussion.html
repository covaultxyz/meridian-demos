Summary

Discussion about Omar opportunity with Ely Beckman

Decisions Made

  • Partnership model: Omar's Millennial company pays for Meridian's architecture/tools access while retaining IP ownership for his own scaling
  • Clear IP boundaries established - neither party will bring competitive solutions to the other
  • Product strategy: deliver customizable terminals per industry rather than selling raw multi-agent architecture
  • Architecture approach: hybrid compute with cloud-based reasoning models plus local lightweight models for scheduling/orchestration
  • Core system includes self-learning capability to identify weak areas, conduct research, and ingest findings into digital brain
  • Implement database-level guardrails to enforce protocol compliance beyond LLM-level constraints
  • First implementation stage: strengthen Omar's existing medical practice through automation and optimization

Commitments

  • Omar: implement the terminal system in his medical practice starting with Stage 1 (practice optimization)
  • Meridian team (Eli/Alex): provide architecture access, tools, and knowledge base to Omar
  • Both parties: work with legal teams to finalize IP agreements and licensing structure

Key Discoveries

  • Database-level constraints are essential - LLMs skip documented protocols unless the database layer enforces them
  • Focused smaller models (70B parameters) with structured databases outperform frontier models for specialized clinical tasks
  • Adoption bottleneck is organizational trust and change speed, not technical capability
  • 95% enterprise AI failure rate stems from misalignment between technology capability and business goals, not technical limitations
