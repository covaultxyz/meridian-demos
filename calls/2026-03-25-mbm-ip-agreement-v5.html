PLAIN-LANGUAGE SUMMARY

*This summary is for the signatories' convenience and does not modify the binding terms below. In the event of any conflict between this summary and the Agreement, the Agreement controls.*

This Agreement establishes:

1. **MBM owns all clinical software** built specifically for MBM's projects — including work already performed since April 2025. This includes all workflows, clinical logic, user interfaces, and configurations designed to MBM's specifications.

2. **Contractor keeps his own tools.** The general-purpose platforms, frameworks, and development tools that Contractor built before this engagement (and continues to develop independently) remain Contractor's property. MBM gets a license to use those tools solely to the extent they are embedded in MBM's software — and a broader operational license ensuring MBM can operate, maintain, and have third parties support the Deliverables after termination.

3. **The Partnership (Mazzei/Bitler) keeps its PaaS product.** The multi-agentic API platform is independent of MBM's software. MBM may choose to become a customer of that platform at arm's-length, but the platform itself is not part of this Agreement.

4. **Two projects are covered:** Project 1 (website automation, no patient data) and Project 2 (clinical psychiatric platform, involves patient data). Project 2 cannot begin until a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement is signed.

5. **Both parties are Act 60 beneficiaries.** The Agreement includes compliance provisions to protect both parties' tax exemption status.

6. **Liability is capped.** Each party's total liability under this Agreement is capped at two times the aggregate fees paid, with specific carve-outs for willful misconduct, IP infringement, and confidentiality breaches.

(e) The cost of the independent attorney shall be shared equally between the Parties.

*Plain-language note: If there is ever a disagreement about whether a specific piece of software belongs to MBM or was Contractor's pre-existing work, this section provides a fast resolution process (15-day discussion, then 30-day expert review) instead of going directly to expensive arbitration. ARTICLE 3 — PROTECTION OF CONTRACTOR'S AND PARTNERSHIP'S INDEPENDENT OPERATIONS

3.1 Preservation of Contractor Pre-Existing IP

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to transfer, assign, limit, or encumber any Contractor Pre-Existing IP. Contractor and the Partnership retain all right, title, and interest in Contractor Pre-Existing IP, and are free to use, license, sell, or otherwise exploit Contractor Pre-Existing IP without restriction. The Parties shall collaboratively maintain Exhibit A as a living inventory of Contractor Pre-Existing IP, updated by mutual written amendment as new items are identified. Any newly identified Contractor Pre-Existing IP shall be added to Exhibit A within thirty (30) days of identification by either Party, and failure to update Exhibit A within this period does not affect the classification of items that otherwise meet the definition in Section 1.2.

3.2 General Knowledge Carve-Out

Nothing in this Agreement shall restrict Contractor's or the Partnership's right to use General Knowledge in any manner, including in connection with services performed for other clients or for Contractor's and the Partnership's own business operations. For the avoidance of doubt, Contractor and the Partnership may freely develop and commercialize their own multi-agentic PaaS platform, tools, and services, provided they do not incorporate, derive from, or depend on any Company IP or MBM Confidential Information.

3.3 No Non-Compete

This Agreement does not impose any non-compete obligation on Contractor or the Partnership. Contractor and the Partnership are free to engage with other clients, including in the healthcare industry, subject only to the confidentiality and IP ownership obligations of this Agreement and the limited Right of First Refusal in Section 4.3. Contractor shall not, however, use MBM's Confidential Information or Company IP in the performance of services for any third party.

3.4 Independent Contractor Tools and Platforms

To the extent Contractor utilizes its own proprietary tools, platforms, frameworks, or software (including tools developed by or through the Partnership) in the performance of Services, such tools shall remain Contractor Pre-Existing IP, provided that:

(a) Such tools are identified in Exhibit A or promptly added thereto by mutual written amendment;

(b) MBM is granted a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use any such tools solely to the extent embedded in or necessary for the operation, maintenance, and modification of the Deliverables, but not for the development of new, standalone products or services unrelated to the Project; and

(c) The Deliverables themselves, including all customizations, configurations, clinical workflows, and business logic layered on top of such tools, are and remain Company IP.

3.5 Operational Continuity License

In addition to the license granted in Section 3.4(b), upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, MBM and its designated successors, contractors, or service providers shall have a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to:

(a) Use, modify, and maintain Contractor Pre-Existing IP components that are embedded in or necessary for the operation of the Deliverables, solely for the purpose of operating, maintaining, supporting, and evolving MBM's Deliverables;

(b) Grant access to such components to replacement contractors or service providers engaged by MBM, provided such third parties are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in Article 5;

(c) Access and use all technical documentation, deployment procedures, configuration guides, and architecture documentation related to the embedded Contractor Pre-Existing IP components, as deposited in the source code escrow under Section 9.6.

This Operational Continuity License does not grant MBM the right to commercialize, sublicense, or distribute Contractor Pre-Existing IP as a standalone product or outside the context of the Deliverables.

*Plain-language note: If Contractor uses a general-purpose deployment tool to build MBM's clinical platform, the tool stays Contractor's, MBM gets a license to use it within their platform, and everything custom-built on top for MBM's clinical needs belongs to MBM. After termination, MBM and any replacement contractor can continue operating and modifying the embedded tools — but cannot resell or repurpose them outside MBM's own platform.*

3.6 Segregation of Work

Both Parties shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable practices to segregate work performed for MBM from work performed for Contractor's own business, the Partnership, or other clients. At minimum, this includes:

(a) Separate code repositories for MBM Project work;

(b) Separate project management environments and communication channels;

(c) Separate access credentials for MBM systems;

(d) Documentation of which personnel have access to MBM materials.

MBM shall provide Contractor with dedicated infrastructure access credentials and isolated development environments for Project work, distinct from MBM's general business systems.

3.7 Layered Architecture Acknowledgment

The Parties acknowledge and agree that the Project architecture consists of distinct layers, and this layered model — as documented in the Technical Architecture Document (Exhibit I) — shall govern the classification of IP components in the event of any ambiguity:

**(a) Infrastructure Layer** (Contractor Pre-Existing IP) — General-purpose platforms, frameworks, development tools, deployment systems, and reusable software components identified in Exhibit A that exist and function independently of the Project. These components were developed before the engagement, are used across multiple clients or projects, and are licensed to MBM per Sections 3.4 and 3.5.

**(b) Application Layer** (Company IP) — All clinical workflows, practice-specific logic, user interfaces designed to MBM's specifications, business rules, clinical decision support logic, screening instruments, patient-facing features, and any other components built specifically for MBM that depend on MBM's specifications, data, or clinical requirements. The Application Layer is Company IP regardless of whether it runs on Infrastructure Layer components.

**(c) Data Layer** (MBM Property) — All patient data, clinical records, practice data, Protected Health Information, and MBM business data. The Data Layer is and remains MBM's exclusive property at all times, subject to HIPAA requirements and the BAA (Exhibit B) where applicable.

**Classification rule**: Components that exist and function independently of the Project are Contractor Pre-Existing IP. Components that were created specifically for MBM and depend on MBM's specifications, data, or clinical requirements are Company IP. When a single component contains elements of both (e.g., a configuration file that adapts a general-purpose framework to MBM's clinical workflow), the general-purpose elements remain Contractor Pre-Existing IP, the MBM-specific customization is Company IP, and either Party may invoke Section 2.8 if the boundary is unclear. The Technical Architecture Document (Exhibit I) provides component-level mapping of these boundaries for all existing Deliverables and shall be updated as new components are created.

ARTICLE 4 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT

4.1 Disclosure Obligation

Contractor shall promptly disclose to MBM any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest that arises during the term of this Agreement, including but not limited to: (a) engagement with any client whose work may overlap with the Project scope as defined in the applicable SOW; (b) development of any product, service, or technology by Contractor or the Partnership that is substantially similar to the specific Project Deliverables; or (c) any situation in which Contractor's duties under this Agreement may conflict with Contractor's or the Partnership's other business interests.

4.2 Good Faith Resolution Process

Upon disclosure of a conflict of interest, the Parties shall engage in a good faith discussion to evaluate and resolve the conflict. Resolution mechanisms may include, but are not limited to: (a) modifying the scope of Services to eliminate the conflict; (b) implementing additional information barriers or segregation protocols; (c) obtaining MBM's written consent to proceed under specified conditions; or (d) any other mutually agreed-upon arrangement.

4.3 MBM's Right of First Refusal

If Contractor or the Partnership develops or contemplates developing any product, service, or technology that is substantially similar to and directly competitive with the specific Project Deliverables described in the applicable Statement of Work, Contractor shall notify MBM and grant MBM a right of first refusal to negotiate a license, acquisition, or collaboration arrangement for such development on commercially reasonable terms.

For the avoidance of doubt, this right of first refusal does not apply to:

(a) General-purpose tools, platforms, or APIs developed by the Partnership that may be used across multiple industries, including healthcare;

(b) Services performed by Contractor or the Partnership for other clients in any industry, including healthcare, provided such services do not incorporate Company IP or MBM Confidential Information;

(c) The Partnership's multi-agentic PaaS API product and related services as described in Exhibit A;

(d) Products or services that serve a different clinical specialty, patient population, or healthcare function than the specific Project Deliverables.

MBM shall exercise or decline its right of first refusal within thirty (30) business days of Contractor's written notice. Failure to respond within this period shall be deemed a waiver of the right of first refusal for the specific development described in the notice.

*Plain-language note: Contractor can freely work in healthcare. This ROFR only triggers if Contractor builds something that directly competes with the specific software built for MBM under this Agreement — not the entire healthcare market.*

4.4 Information Barriers

Where Contractor or the Partnership simultaneously serves MBM and other clients in related domains, Contractor shall implement effective information barriers ("ethical walls") to prevent the cross-contamination of Confidential Information between engagements. Such barriers shall include, at minimum: (a) logical and, where feasible, physical separation of project materials; (b) access controls limiting personnel access to information on a need-to-know basis; and (c) documented procedures for handling conflicts.

The Parties shall use commercially reasonable efforts to finalize and execute the BAA within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date. If the BAA is not executed within ninety (90) days of the Effective Date, either Party may request a good faith discussion to address any outstanding issues.

EXHIBIT D — LEGAL BEST PRACTICE RECOMMENDATIONS

*The following recommendations are provided to help all Parties prevent, minimize, and manage potential legal conflicts. These are strategic suggestions and should be reviewed with qualified legal counsel.*

D.1 For MBM (Highest Priority)

**Register IP Early.** File provisional patent applications and copyright registrations for key software components as early as feasible. Registration creates a public record and statutory presumption of ownership.

**Maintain an IP Ledger.** Keep a running log of all Company IP created, with dates, descriptions, and the personnel involved. This establishes a clear chain of title.

**Keep Time Records.** Detailed time logs showing what work was done for MBM vs. the Partnership protect against future disputes about what was created "in scope" of this Agreement.

**Flag Overlaps Proactively.** If the Partnership develops a feature or tool that might overlap with MBM work, raise it immediately under Section 4.1. Early disclosure prevents small issues from becoming legal disputes.
