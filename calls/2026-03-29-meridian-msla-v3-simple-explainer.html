Meridian MSLA v3 — Simple Breakdown

What's New in v3?

V2 set up the four license tiers (Standard, Extended, Enterprise, Buyout) but didn't put prices on the menu. V3 adds the pricing framework — now every tier has a defined cost structure, and there's a new option for clients who want exclusivity in their local market.

Think of v2 as the restaurant posting its menu with dish descriptions but no prices. V3 adds the prices.

---

The Two Big Additions

1. License Tier Pricing (Section 3.5)

Every tier now has a price — or more accurately, a pricing *method* that gets negotiated per project. Here's how it works:

#### Tier 1 — Standard License: FREE (included)

No extra charge beyond the hourly service rates. You pay for the work, you get basic internal-use rights. This is the default.

**Analogy**: You buy a meal, and eating it is included in the price.

#### Tier 2 — Extended License: Premium

You want to let your affiliated companies use the software, hire a different tech firm to maintain it, or move it to different servers. That flexibility costs extra.

**Three ways to pay** (negotiated per project):

| Option | How It Works | Best For |

|--------|-------------|----------|

| **20% surcharge** | Every invoice is 20% higher | Short projects billed hourly |

| **$15,000 - $30,000 per year** | Flat annual fee, paid upfront | Ongoing relationships |

| **25% of project value** | One-time payment when the project starts | Fixed-scope projects |

**Analogy**: You're upgrading from a regular car rental to one where you can add extra drivers, drive across state lines, and take it to any mechanic.

#### Tier 3 — Enterprise License: Major Premium

You want to sublicense the software to your own customers, create new versions, or include it in a sale of your company. This is a commercial-use license.

**Four ways to pay** (negotiated per project):

| Option | How It Works | Best For |

|--------|-------------|----------|

| **40% surcharge** | Every invoice is 40% higher | Short projects |

| **$40,000 - $100,000 per year** | Flat annual fee | Established platform relationships |

| **30% upfront + 4% of revenue** | You pay less now, but share revenue quarterly when you sublicense | Clients who plan to resell |

| **15% upfront + 2% of exit value** | Reduced upfront, but Meridian gets a small slice if you sell the company within 3 years | Pre-revenue or startup clients |

**Important cap**: Revenue sharing or success fees stop after 5 years OR after you've paid 2x the original project cost — whichever comes first. After that, your license is fully paid forever.

**Analogy**: You're licensing a franchise. You can operate it, customize it, even open new locations — but the franchisor gets a cut until the license is paid off.

#### Tier 4 — Buyout: Separate Deal

You want to actually *own* specific pieces of technology. This is a separate negotiation with a separate price.

**How the price is set**:

• **Development cost multiplier**: 3-5 times what it cost to build (hours x rate)

• **Independent appraisal**: A third-party appraiser determines fair market value

• **Negotiated flat fee**: Both sides agree on a number

**Minimums**:

• No buyout for less than $50,000 (or 3x development cost, whichever is higher)

• If you want Meridian to *stop using* the technology too (exclusivity), that doubles the price

**Analogy**: You're buying the recipe itself, not just the meal. And if you want the chef to promise never to cook that dish for anyone else, that costs twice as much.

---

2. Exclusivity Option (Section 3.6)

By default, Meridian can work with anyone — including your competitors. That's the standard deal.

But if you want Meridian to NOT build similar software for your competitors in your area, you can buy **field-limited exclusivity**. It's like a non-compete agreement, but for Meridian's services — and it has a price tag.

#### The Three Rules of Exclusivity

Exclusivity must be:

1. **Specific to your field** — "outpatient psychiatric practices," not "all healthcare"

2. **Limited to your area** — "San Juan metro," not "the whole world"

3. **Time-limited** — 24 months maximum, then you renew or it expires

#### What It Costs

| How Specific | Monthly | Annual |

|-------------|---------|--------|

| **Narrow** (your specialty + your metro area) | $2,000 - $4,000 | $20,000 - $40,000 |

| **Medium** (your specialty + your territory, like all of Puerto Rico) | $4,000 - $8,000 | $40,000 - $75,000 |

| **Broad** (your category + national) | $8,000 - $15,000 | $80,000 - $150,000 |

#### What Exclusivity Does NOT Restrict

Even with exclusivity, Meridian can still:

• Use its own platform and tools for any client in any market

• Sell the AgentVox PaaS product to anyone

• Work with clients outside your field or area

• Work with clients in your area who don't need similar software

Exclusivity only covers the specific custom software (Client Deliverables) listed in the agreement — not Meridian's general technology.

#### What Happens If You Don't Pay

If you miss a payment and don't fix it within 15 days, exclusivity ends automatically. Meridian is free to work with anyone again.

#### Why This Is Smart for Both Sides

**For the Client**: You protect your local competitive advantage. No one else in your market gets the same custom software.

**For Meridian**: You get paid for the business you're turning away. If you agree not to serve competitors in San Juan, that's real revenue you're giving up — the exclusivity fee compensates for that.

**For both**: It's time-limited and renewable. Nobody is locked in forever. If the relationship is working, you renew. If not, it expires naturally.

Updated Project Plan (SOW Template)

The project plan form now includes two new sections:

**Section 8 — License Tier Fee**: Which tier, which pricing model, how much, when it's due.

**Section 9 — Exclusivity Option**: Whether exclusivity is requested, and if so: what field, what geography, how long, and how much per month/year.

These are separate line items from the hourly service fees. The total engagement cost is now:

> **Total = Service fees + License tier fee + Exclusivity fee (if any)**

A Note on Pricing

The prices in this agreement are **ranges, not fixed numbers**. The actual price is negotiated for each project based on:

• How big the project is

• How much the software is worth to the client's business

• How broad the rights are (more rights = higher price)

• For exclusivity: how much business Meridian gives up by not serving competitors

Think of it like real estate: the listing has a price range, and the final number depends on the specific deal.

The Bottom Line (Updated for v3)

Everything from v2 still applies. Here's what v3 adds:

1. **The menu has prices now.** Each tier has a defined cost structure — percentage surcharge, flat annual fee, one-time license fee, revenue share, or success fee.

2. **Broader rights cost more.** Tier 1 is free. Tier 2 adds ~20%. Tier 3 adds ~40% or includes revenue sharing. Tier 4 is a separate buyout deal with a $50K floor.

3. **Revenue sharing has a cap.** If you're on a revenue-share or success-fee model, it stops after 5 years or 2x the project cost — then your license is fully paid forever.

4. **Exclusivity is available but priced separately.** You can protect your local market, but you pay a monthly/annual fee for it. Meridian gets compensated for the business it turns away.

5. **Exclusivity has limits.** It's bounded by specialty, geography, and time. It doesn't restrict Meridian's platform, general tools, or work outside your market.

6. **If you stop paying for exclusivity, it ends.** 15-day cure period, then automatic termination. No ongoing obligation for either side.

7. **The project plan now captures everything.** Service fees, license tier, exclusivity — all in one SOW with clear line items.
