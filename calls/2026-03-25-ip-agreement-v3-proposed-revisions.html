Ip Agreement V3 Proposed Revisions

CLIENT 2026-03-25 alex

Summary

Proposed revisions to MBM contractor IP agreement closing ambiguity gaps through detailed definitions, dispute processes, layered architecture codification, and security standards while preserving MBM's absolute company IP ownership.

Decisions Made

  • Define 'MBM Resources' to exclude general-purpose equipment and infrastructure from IP capture scope
  • Implement IP classification dispute fast-track with independent attorney review instead of arbitration (§2.7)
  • Codify three-layer architecture model (Infrastructure/Application/Data) as governing framework for IP classification
  • Narrow right of first refusal to Project-specific competition only, explicitly excluding Partnership's multi-agent PaaS platform
  • Add Phase 2 HIPAA gate requiring executed BAA before any PHI-related work or Project 2 commencement (§5.5)
  • Revise attorney-in-fact powers to exclude disputed IP and terminate automatically on agreement end (§2.5)
  • Add MBM security standards representation and warranty addressing infrastructure and incident disclosure (§7.6)
  • Change Exhibit E transfer pricing framework from unilateral imposition to mutual activation with 30-day review period

Commitments

  • Contractor to execute IP assignment documents within 30 business days of MBM request (§2.5)
  • Parties to attempt IP classification dispute resolution through good faith discussion within 15 business days (§2.7)
  • Independent IP attorney to provide classification recommendation within 30 days of engagement (§2.7)
  • MBM to exercise or decline right of first refusal within 30 business days of Contractor notice (§4.3)
  • MBM to approve/deny subcontractors within 10 business days with deemed approval if no response (§6.4)
  • MBM to disclose all active or recently resolved security incidents prior to Contractor tool deployment (§7.6)
  • Either party to provide 60 days written notice before non-renewal (§9.1)
  • Activating party to provide minimum 30 days review period for Exhibit E transfer pricing framework before implementation

Key Discoveries

  • Exhibit A was completely unpopulated—single largest risk gap in agreement, enabling IP ownership disputes
  • Verbal March 10 IP framework agreement was never codified, creating conflicting interpretations of what constitutes Company IP
  • PHI/HIPAA work had no explicit compliance gate, potentially enabling non-compliant clinical work before BAA execution
  • Right of first refusal was broad enough to block the Partnership's entire healthcare market addressable market
  • MBM infrastructure compromise (active GCP compromise mentioned) exposed contractor tool/credential security risks that agreement previously didn't address
