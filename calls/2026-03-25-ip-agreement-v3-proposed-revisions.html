Decisions Made

  • Define 'MBM Resources' to exclude general-purpose equipment and infrastructure from IP capture scope

  • Codify three-layer architecture model (Infrastructure/Application/Data) as governing framework for IP classification
  • Narrow right of first refusal to Project-specific competition only, explicitly excluding Partnership's multi-agent PaaS platform
  • Add Phase 2 HIPAA gate requiring executed BAA before any PHI-related work or Project 2 commencement (§5.5)
  • Revise attorney-in-fact powers to exclude disputed IP and terminate automatically on agreement end (§2.5)
  • Add MBM security standards representation and warranty addressing infrastructure and incident disclosure (§7.6)
  • Change Exhibit E transfer pricing framework from unilateral imposition to mutual activation with 30-day review period

Commitments

  • Contractor to execute IP assignment documents within 30 business days of MBM request (§2.5)

MBM IP Agreement v3 — Proposed Revisions

**Date**: 2026-03-24

**Source**: Lens + Prism Coherence Audit

**Scope**: All sections requiring revision for fair, bilateral terms

**Intent**: Preserve MBM's absolute ownership of Company IP while closing ambiguity gaps that could harm either party

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Company IP" (revised)

"Company IP" means all intellectual property, inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, designs, algorithms, software code (source and object), documentation, specifications, architectures, system designs, data models, schemas, workflows, user interfaces, trade secrets, know-how, methodologies, processes, techniques, and any other proprietary materials, whether or not patentable or copyrightable, that are (a) conceived, created, developed, reduced to practice, or derived in whole or in part in connection with the Services performed under this Agreement; (b) based on, derived from, or incorporating MBM's Confidential Information; or (c) created using MBM Resources (as defined in Section 1.8), data, or specifications.

1.8 "MBM Resources" (new definition)

"MBM Resources" means MBM's proprietary software, proprietary data sets, patient data, clinical protocols, practice-specific workflows, and documented technical specifications specific to the Project. MBM Resources does not include general-purpose computing infrastructure, cloud hosting services, development tools, communication platforms, or other resources that are not specific to MBM's proprietary business operations.

ARTICLE 2 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY OWNERSHIP AND ASSIGNMENT

2.5 Further Assurances (revised)

Contractor agrees to execute any and all documents, instruments, and agreements, and to take any and all actions, that MBM may reasonably request to evidence, perfect, register, or enforce MBM's rights in the Company IP, including but not limited to patent applications, copyright registrations, assignments, declarations, and powers of attorney.

In the event Contractor fails or refuses to execute such documents within thirty (30) business days of MBM's written request, Contractor hereby appoints MBM as Contractor's attorney-in-fact to execute such documents on Contractor's behalf, subject to the following limitations:

(a) This power of attorney shall not apply to any intellectual property that is the subject of an active dispute between the Parties regarding its classification as Company IP, Contractor Pre-Existing IP, or General Knowledge;

(b) This power of attorney shall terminate upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement;

(c) MBM shall provide Contractor with copies of any documents executed under this power of attorney within five (5) business days of execution.

2.7 IP Classification Disputes (new section)

In the event of a dispute regarding whether specific intellectual property constitutes Company IP, Contractor Pre-Existing IP, or General Knowledge, the Parties shall:

(a) First attempt to resolve the dispute through good faith discussion within fifteen (15) business days of either Party raising the dispute in writing;

(b) If unresolved, engage an independent intellectual property attorney, mutually agreed upon by both Parties, to review the disputed IP and provide a written classification recommendation within thirty (30) days of engagement;

(c) The independent attorney's recommendation shall be binding on both Parties unless either Party initiates formal dispute resolution under Section 10.1 within fifteen (15) business days of receiving the recommendation;

(d) During the pendency of any IP classification dispute, neither Party shall use, license, transfer, or encumber the disputed IP without the other Party's written consent;

(e) The cost of the independent attorney shall be shared equally between the Parties.

ARTICLE 3 — PROTECTION OF CONTRACTOR'S AND PARTNERSHIP'S INDEPENDENT OPERATIONS

3.4 Independent Contractor Tools and Platforms (revised)

To the extent Contractor utilizes its own proprietary tools, platforms, frameworks, or software (including tools developed by or through the Partnership) in the performance of Services, such tools shall remain Contractor Pre-Existing IP, provided that: (a) such tools are identified in Exhibit A or promptly added thereto; (b) MBM is granted a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use any such tools solely to the extent embedded in or necessary for the operation, maintenance, and modification of the Deliverables, but not for the development of new, standalone products or services unrelated to the Project; and (c) the Deliverables themselves, including all customizations, configurations, and business logic layered on top of such tools, are and remain Company IP.

3.5 Segregation of Work (revised)

Both Parties shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable practices to segregate work performed for MBM from work performed for Contractor's own business, the Partnership, or other clients. This includes maintaining separate code repositories, project management environments, documentation systems, and access credentials. MBM shall provide Contractor with dedicated infrastructure access credentials and isolated development environments for Project work, distinct from MBM's general business systems.

3.6 Layered Architecture Acknowledgment (new section)

The Parties acknowledge that the Project architecture consists of distinct layers:

(a) **Infrastructure Layer** — Contractor Pre-Existing IP (including but not limited to the tools, platforms, and frameworks identified in Exhibit A), licensed to MBM per Section 3.4;

(b) **Application Layer** — All clinical workflows, practice-specific logic, user interfaces designed to MBM's specifications, and business rules built on top of the Infrastructure Layer, which constitute Company IP;

(c) **Data Layer** — All patient data, clinical records, practice data, and Protected Health Information, which are and remain MBM's exclusive property at all times.

This layered model shall govern the classification of IP components in the event of any ambiguity. Components that exist and function independently of the Project (Infrastructure Layer) are Contractor Pre-Existing IP. Components that were created specifically for MBM and depend on MBM's specifications, data, or clinical requirements (Application Layer and Data Layer) are Company IP.

ARTICLE 4 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT

4.3 MBM's Right of First Refusal (revised)

If Contractor or the Partnership develops or contemplates developing any product, service, or technology that is substantially similar to and directly competitive with the specific Project Deliverables described in the applicable Statement of Work, Contractor shall notify MBM and grant MBM a right of first refusal to negotiate a license, acquisition, or collaboration arrangement for such development on commercially reasonable terms.

For the avoidance of doubt, this right of first refusal does not apply to: (a) general-purpose tools, platforms, or APIs developed by the Partnership that may be used across multiple industries, including healthcare; (b) services performed by Contractor or the Partnership for other clients in any industry, including healthcare, provided such services do not incorporate Company IP or MBM Confidential Information; or (c) the Partnership's multi-agentic PaaS API product and related services as described in Exhibit A.

MBM shall exercise or decline its right of first refusal within thirty (30) business days of Contractor's written notice. Failure to respond within this period shall be deemed a waiver of the right of first refusal for the specific development described in the notice.

---

ARTICLE 5 — CONFIDENTIALITY

5.5 HIPAA Compliance (revised, with Phase 2 gate)

To the extent Contractor may access, receive, create, or maintain Protected Health Information ("PHI") as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended ("HIPAA"), and its implementing regulations, the Parties shall execute a separate Business Associate Agreement ("BAA") substantially in the form attached as Exhibit B, which shall govern the treatment of PHI. In the event of any conflict between the confidentiality provisions of this Agreement and the BAA, the BAA shall control with respect to PHI.

**No Services related to Project 2 (Clinical Platform) or any engagement involving access to, creation of, or processing of PHI shall commence until Exhibit B (BAA) has been fully executed by both Parties.** This gate applies regardless of whether the Agreement has been signed and is in effect.

---

ARTICLE 6 — INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

6.4 Subcontracting (revised)

Contractor may utilize personnel from the Partnership to perform Services under this Agreement, subject to the following conditions: (a) MBM's prior written approval of each individual, which shall not be unreasonably withheld and shall be provided or denied within ten (10) business days of Contractor's written request — failure to respond within this period shall be deemed approval; (b) each such individual executes a written agreement containing IP assignment and confidentiality provisions at least as protective as those in this Agreement, assigning all rights in Company IP directly to MBM or to Contractor for subsequent assignment to MBM; and (c) Contractor remains primarily responsible for the performance of all Services and all obligations under this Agreement.

---

ARTICLE 7 — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

7.6 Security Standards (new section)

MBM represents and warrants that it shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable security measures for all infrastructure, systems, and environments on which Deliverables are deployed or through which Contractor accesses MBM systems, including but not limited to: (a) industry-standard access controls and authentication; (b) encryption of data in transit and at rest; (c) regular security assessments and timely remediation of identified vulnerabilities; and (d) prompt notification to Contractor of any security incident that affects or may affect the Deliverables, Contractor's tools, or Contractor's access credentials.

MBM shall disclose to Contractor any known, active, or recently resolved security incidents affecting the infrastructure on which Deliverables will be deployed, prior to Contractor deploying any tools or software to such infrastructure.

9.1 Term (revised)

Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of twelve (12) months (each a "Renewal Term") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term.
