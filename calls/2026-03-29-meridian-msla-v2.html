Decisions Made

  • Meridian retains all background IP ownership; transfers only expressly-identified Client Deliverables by license tier

  • Client Deliverables must be expressly identified in SOW with specificity; absent identification defaults to Meridian Background IP

**Date**: 2026-03-29

PREAMBLE — INTENT AND OWNERSHIP FRAMEWORK

Section 1.1 Purpose and Intent

The purpose of this Agreement is to define the terms under which Meridian will provide certain software development, architecture, consulting, integration, and related services to Client, while preserving Meridian's ownership of its intellectual property and clearly identifying any limited rights Client may receive in specifically contracted deliverables.

The Parties acknowledge and agree that Meridian owns and shall retain all right, title, and interest in and to its pre-existing and independently developed intellectual property, including without limitation all software, source code, object code, tools, frameworks, libraries, infrastructure, architectures, methods, workflows, know-how, documentation, templates, automations, models, systems, trade secrets, improvements, modifications, and derivative works created, owned, or controlled by Meridian before, outside of, or independently from the specific Services performed under an executed Statement of Work.

Nothing in this Agreement is intended to assign, transfer, convey, encumber, or license Meridian's core technology, reusable systems, platform components, general development assets, methods, or know-how to Client except to the limited extent expressly stated in a fully executed Statement of Work or other written amendment signed by both Parties and supported by separately negotiated consideration where applicable.

The Parties further acknowledge that Meridian is not entering into this Agreement for the purpose of making an absolute or unconditional transfer of all intellectual property rights. Any ownership transfer, exclusive license, perpetual license, buyout, or other extraordinary grant of rights must be expressly described in a written agreement signed by both Parties, must identify the specific intellectual property covered, and must be supported by clearly stated commercial terms.

For the avoidance of doubt, payment for Services, collaboration on a project, access to Client systems, use of Client data, use of Client specifications, or delivery of work product shall not by itself operate to transfer ownership of Meridian's background technology, reusable components, platform assets, general tools, methodologies, or independently valuable systems.

Section 1.2 Definitions

**"Services"** means the software development, architecture, consulting, integration, deployment, maintenance, support, and related professional services to be performed by Meridian for Client as described in an executed Statement of Work.

**"Confidential Information"** means any and all non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or through observation, including but not limited to: business plans, strategies, financial data, pricing, customer lists, technical specifications, source code, algorithms, trade secrets, product roadmaps, marketing strategies, and any information designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was rightfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

Section 1.3 What This Agreement Is NOT

This Agreement is not:

• An IP assignment agreement — Meridian does not assign its core technology

• A work-for-hire arrangement — Meridian is an independent technology company, not an employee

• A blanket transfer of rights — nothing passes by implication, estoppel, or course of dealing

• A buyout — acquisition of Meridian's platform or technology requires separate negotiation and separate consideration

ARTICLE 2 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY OWNERSHIP

Section 2.1 Meridian Background IP

"Meridian Background IP" means all intellectual property, technology, materials, and proprietary rights owned, developed, conceived, reduced to practice, authored, or controlled by Meridian or its personnel, whether alone or with others, other than expressly identified Client Deliverables created exclusively under an executed Statement of Work. Meridian Background IP includes, without limitation, all pre-existing and independently developed software, code, libraries, APIs, frameworks, infrastructure, deployment systems, orchestration systems, security tooling, reusable modules, data models, designs, documentation, methods, processes, techniques, architectures, general know-how, and all improvements, modifications, updates, and derivatives thereof.

Meridian Background IP shall remain the exclusive property of Meridian. Client acquires no ownership interest in Meridian Background IP by implication, estoppel, course of dealing, access, payment, integration, embedding, customization, or otherwise.

Section 2.2 Client Deliverables

"Client Deliverables" means only those specific deliverables that are expressly identified as Client Deliverables in an executed Statement of Work and that are created by Meridian specifically and exclusively for Client in the course of performing the Services under that Statement of Work.

No work product shall be deemed a Client Deliverable unless the applicable Statement of Work expressly identifies it with reasonable specificity. In the absence of such identification, the work product shall be deemed Meridian Background IP or Meridian work product licensed on a non-exclusive basis, as applicable.

Client Deliverables may include: custom clinical workflows, practice-specific business rules, user interfaces designed to Client's specifications, client-specific configurations, and client-facing application logic. Client Deliverables do not include: Meridian's underlying platform, frameworks, libraries, orchestration systems, deployment tooling, reusable code, general-purpose modules, or any component that Meridian uses or intends to use across multiple clients or projects.

Section 2.3 No Implied Assignment

Except as expressly stated in a signed writing, nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as:

(a) an assignment of Meridian Background IP;

(b) an assignment of any improvements, modifications, or derivative works of Meridian Background IP;

(c) a transfer of ownership in any architecture, framework, library, integration method, deployment method, or reusable component used in performing the Services; or

(d) an exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, or transferable grant of rights in favor of Client.

Any rights granted to Client in Meridian technology shall be by license only, shall be limited to the scope expressly stated in the applicable Statement of Work, and shall not include the right to resell, sublicense, reverse engineer, extract, reuse outside the project scope, or claim ownership of Meridian Background IP unless expressly agreed in writing.

Section 2.4 Improvements and Derivatives

Any and all improvements, enhancements, updates, modifications, adaptations, derivative works, and extensions of Meridian Background IP, whether developed before, during, or after the term of this Agreement, shall remain Meridian Background IP unless a specific item is expressly designated in a signed writing as a Client-owned Deliverable.

To the extent any Client Deliverable incorporates, interfaces with, depends on, or is built upon Meridian Background IP, Client shall receive only the limited rights expressly granted in the applicable Statement of Work, and Meridian shall retain ownership of all underlying and reusable technology.

Section 2.5 Pre-SOW and Informal Work

If Services are performed before a Statement of Work is signed, or if work is performed outside the scope of an executed Statement of Work, all resulting work product shall be deemed Meridian Background IP unless and until the Parties later agree otherwise in a signed writing that specifically identifies the work product and the rights being granted.

If Client later incorporates pre-SOW work into a project governed by an executed Statement of Work, the Parties may execute a written ratification identifying the specific pre-SOW work product and its classification as either a Client Deliverable or Meridian Background IP licensed to Client.

Section 2.6 Client Data

All data provided by Client, generated by Client's operations, or relating to Client's patients, customers, or business ("Client Data") shall remain Client's exclusive property at all times. Meridian shall not use Client Data for any purpose other than performing the Services under an executed Statement of Work. Upon termination, Meridian shall return or destroy Client Data in accordance with Article 9.

To the extent Client Data includes Protected Health Information ("PHI"), the handling of such data shall be governed by a separately executed Business Associate Agreement (Exhibit B).

ARTICLE 3 — LICENSE GRANTS

Section 3.1 License Tiers

The rights Client receives in Meridian technology are determined by the license tier specified in the applicable Statement of Work. Unless a SOW specifies otherwise, Client receives a Standard License (Tier 1).

| Tier | Name | What Client Gets | What Client Does NOT Get |

|------|------|-----------------|------------------------|

| **Tier 1** | Standard License | Non-exclusive, non-transferable, internal-use-only license to use Client Deliverables and embedded Meridian Background IP solely for Client's own internal business operations. | No sublicensing, no resale, no white-labeling, no modification of Meridian Background IP, no use outside Client's organization. |

| **Tier 2** | Extended License | Everything in Tier 1, plus: right to allow affiliates to use, right to engage third-party contractors to maintain/modify Client Deliverables (including embedded Meridian Background IP), right to migrate to replacement infrastructure. | No sublicensing to unrelated third parties, no resale, no white-labeling. |

| **Tier 3** | Enterprise License | Everything in Tier 2, plus: right to sublicense to Client's customers in connection with Client's own products/services, right to create derivative works of Client Deliverables, right to assign to acquirers/successors. | No independent commercialization of Meridian Background IP. No right to sell, license, or distribute Meridian's platform, tools, or frameworks as standalone products. |

| **Tier 4** | Buyout / Assignment | Transfer of ownership of specifically identified Client Deliverables. Requires a separate Buyout Agreement (Exhibit G) with separately negotiated consideration. Meridian retains a non-exclusive license to use any transferred technology in its own operations and for other clients unless expressly waived. | Scope limited to specifically identified items in the Buyout Agreement. Does NOT include Meridian Background IP unless expressly listed and separately priced. |

> **How this works**: The SOW says "License Tier: 2" (or whatever is negotiated). The pricing reflects the tier — broader rights cost more. This replaces the old model of "absolute assignment with carve-outs" with "Meridian keeps everything, Client buys defined access."

Section 3.2 License Terms Common to All Tiers

All licenses granted under this Agreement are:

(a) **Perpetual** — the license does not expire upon termination of this Agreement, provided Client has paid all fees due under the applicable Statement of Work;

(b) **Royalty-free** — no ongoing royalty payments are required after payment of the fees specified in the applicable Statement of Work (except for Tier 4, which may include ongoing royalties as specified in the Buyout Agreement);

(c) **Non-exclusive** (Tiers 1-3) — Meridian retains the right to use the same technology, methods, and approaches for other clients and its own operations;

(d) **Irrevocable upon full payment** — once Client has paid all fees due under the applicable Statement of Work in full, the license granted under that SOW becomes fully vested and irrevocable, and cannot be suspended or revoked by Meridian except in the event of Client's material breach of the IP ownership provisions (Article 2) or confidentiality obligations (Article 6) that is not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice;

(e) **Conditional during payment period** — prior to full payment of all fees due under an applicable SOW, license rights are conditioned on Client's continued payment. If Client fails to pay fees due and does not cure within thirty (30) days of written notice, Meridian may suspend the license until payment is received. For the avoidance of doubt, suspension under this subsection is a temporary remedy during the cure period — it does not constitute revocation.

Section 3.3 Embedded Meridian Background IP

To the extent Client Deliverables incorporate, interface with, or depend on Meridian Background IP, Client receives a license to use that embedded Meridian Background IP solely to the extent necessary to exercise the rights granted under the applicable license tier. This license does not extend to:

(a) use of the embedded Meridian Background IP independently of the Client Deliverables;

(b) extraction, reverse engineering, or isolation of Meridian Background IP from the Client Deliverables;

(c) any use beyond the scope of the applicable license tier.

Section 3.4 Separate Buyout or Expanded Rights

If Client desires ownership of any intellectual property beyond specifically identified Client Deliverables, or desires an exclusive, perpetual, transferable, or buyout-style grant of rights in any Meridian technology, the Parties shall negotiate a separate Buyout Agreement (Exhibit G) setting forth:

(i) the exact assets covered;

(ii) whether the grant is exclusive or non-exclusive;

(iii) whether derivative works and improvements are included;

(iv) any field-of-use, territory, or term limitations;

(v) the separate license fee, assignment fee, or other consideration payable for such rights; and

(vi) whether Meridian retains a license-back for its own use.

Unless and until such Buyout Agreement is executed, Meridian retains all right, title, and interest in such intellectual property.

ARTICLE 4 — COMMERCIAL TERMS

Section 4.1 Statements of Work Required

No Services shall begin unless the Parties have executed a Statement of Work describing, at minimum:

(a) the scope of Services;

(b) the commercial terms and pricing (hourly rates, fixed fees, milestones, or hybrid);

(c) the payment terms, including deposit requirements;

(d) the specific deliverables, identified as Client Deliverables or Meridian work product as applicable;

(f) the license tier (Tier 1-4) applicable to the engagement;

(g) whether the SOW involves Protected Health Information (and if so, the BAA must be executed first);

(h) the timeline and milestones;

(i) any Meridian Background IP expected to be used (for transparency, not transfer).

Section 4.2 Deposits and Payment

Client shall pay a deposit as specified in the applicable Statement of Work before Meridian begins Services. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW:

(a) **Deposit**: Fifty percent (50%) of the estimated total engagement value, or the first milestone payment, whichever is applicable, due upon SOW execution;

(b) **Progress payments**: Billed monthly at the rates specified in Exhibit C, due within thirty (30) days of invoice;

(c) **Milestone payments**: If the SOW uses milestone-based pricing, each milestone payment is due upon Meridian's delivery and Client's acceptance (or deemed acceptance) of the milestone deliverable;

(d) **Late payments**: Accrue interest at the lesser of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, commencing on the thirty-first (31st) day after receipt of a properly submitted invoice;

(e) **Disputed invoices**: Client must dispute in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt. Undisputed portions must be paid on time. Deemed acceptance of an invoice is for payment purposes only and does not constitute acceptance of the underlying work product (which is governed by the acceptance criteria in the SOW).

ARTICLE 5 — DELIVERABLE ACCEPTANCE AND WARRANTY

Section 5.1 Acceptance Testing

Client shall have twenty (20) business days following delivery of each deliverable to review and either accept or provide written notice of deficiencies.

For **Major Releases** (initial platform launches, security-sensitive releases, PHI-handling integrations, or deliverables exceeding 100 hours of billed development), Client's express written acceptance is required. Deemed acceptance does not apply to Major Releases.

For all other deliverables, if Client does not provide written notice of acceptance or deficiency within the review period, the deliverable is deemed accepted. If Client provides notice of deficiencies, Meridian shall cure within ten (10) business days at no additional cost, and Client shall have ten (10) additional business days to re-review.

If Client is actively conducting acceptance testing at the end of the review period and provides written notice that testing is ongoing, the review period is automatically extended by ten (10) business days.

Section 5.2 Warranty

For a period of ninety (90) days following acceptance of each deliverable, Meridian shall correct at no additional cost:

(a) material defects or nonconformities with the applicable acceptance criteria;

(b) latent defects — defects that could not reasonably have been discovered during acceptance testing through normal testing procedures;

(c) security vulnerabilities in Meridian-written code discovered through security assessment or penetration testing;

(d) failures to meet documented specifications that manifest during normal business use.

The warranty period for defects corrected under this Section shall restart for the corrected component for thirty (30) days from delivery of the correction.

Section 5.3 Post-Warranty Support

After the warranty period, Meridian may provide ongoing support and maintenance under a separate Support Agreement or as specified in the applicable SOW. Post-warranty support is not included in the base Services fee unless expressly stated.

ARTICLE 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION

Section 6.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" is defined in Section 1.2. Each Party agrees to hold in confidence the other Party's Confidential Information and to use it only for the purposes of this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care. Confidential Information may be disclosed only to personnel and subcontractors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement. The confidentiality obligations under this Section 6.1 shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years from the date of disclosure, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for as long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

Section 6.2 Client Data and PHI

Client Data remains Client's exclusive property. If Services involve Protected Health Information, the Parties shall execute a Business Associate Agreement (Exhibit B) before any PHI-related work begins.

Each Statement of Work shall include a PHI Classification determining whether the engagement involves PHI. If the SOW determines PHI is involved, the BAA is a precondition to commencement. If PHI is discovered mid-engagement despite a negative SOW classification, work involving PHI is immediately suspended until the BAA is executed.

Section 6.3 Security

Both Parties shall implement commercially reasonable security measures. Each Party shall notify the other in writing within seventy-two (72) hours of discovery of any security incident affecting the other Party's data, systems, or technology.

Section 8.2 Indemnification

Each Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the other Party from third-party claims arising from (a) the indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement, (b) the indemnifying Party's negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) infringement of third-party intellectual property rights by materials provided by the indemnifying Party.

ARTICLE 9 — TERM, TERMINATION, AND TRANSITION

Section 9.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated. Individual SOWs have their own terms as specified therein.

Section 9.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon sixty (60) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately upon material breach that is not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice.

Section 9.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

(a) All licenses granted under executed SOWs that have been fully paid survive termination in accordance with their terms;

(b) Meridian shall deliver all Client Deliverables and Client Data in a standard, machine-readable format;

(c) Client's license to Meridian Background IP embedded in Client Deliverables survives per the applicable license tier, provided all fees are paid;

(d) Meridian shall provide transition assistance for ninety (90) days at Tier 1 rates, including knowledge transfer (minimum forty (40) hours) and cooperation with replacement contractors;

(e) Meridian Background IP remains Meridian's exclusive property.

Section 9.4 Continuous Access During Term

Throughout the term, Meridian shall provide Client with access to:

(a) current source code for Client Deliverables in a version-controlled repository;

(b) build instructions, deployment scripts, and dependency inventories sufficient for Client or a third party to independently deploy and operate the Client Deliverables;

(c) administrative credentials for Client-owned infrastructure;

(d) a current dependency inventory listing all third-party tools and services required to operate the Client Deliverables.

Meridian shall update these materials within ten (10) business days of any material change.

Section 9.5 Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent, except that Client may assign in connection with a merger or acquisition upon thirty (30) days' notice. If Client assigns to an entity that is a direct competitor of Meridian in the multi-agentic PaaS market (as mutually determined, or by independent determination per Section 10.3), Meridian may provide notice of intent to terminate, subject to a one hundred eighty (180)-day Transition Period during which Meridian shall continue Services, provide knowledge transfer, and cooperate with replacement contractors. All paid licenses survive.

ARTICLE 10 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 10.1 Independent Contractor

Meridian is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or joint venturer of Client. Meridian controls the manner and means of performing the Services.

Section 10.1A Subcontracting

Meridian may utilize subcontractors, including personnel from the Partnership (as defined in the applicable SOW), to perform Services under this Agreement, subject to the following conditions: (a) Client's prior written approval of each individual who will have access to Client Data or PHI, which shall not be unreasonably withheld and shall be provided or denied within ten (10) business days of Meridian's written request — failure to respond within this period shall be deemed approval; (b) each such individual executes a written agreement containing IP assignment (to Meridian) and confidentiality provisions at least as protective as those in this Agreement; (c) for engagements involving PHI, each subcontractor with PHI access shall be bound by the BAA (Exhibit B) or a substantially equivalent agreement; and (d) Meridian remains primarily responsible for the performance of all Services and all obligations under this Agreement.

Section 10.2 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, without regard to conflicts of law principles. Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in San Juan, Puerto Rico under the rules of JAMS.

Section 10.3 Dispute Resolution

Disputes regarding IP classification, license scope, or deliverable acceptance shall first be subject to good-faith discussion (15 business days), then independent expert determination (binding below $50,000), then formal arbitration. Cost-shifting applies if the escalating party loses on the same grounds as the expert recommendation.

Section 10.4 Moral Rights (Puerto Rico)

The Parties acknowledge that Puerto Rico Act 55-2012 recognizes moral rights as non-waivable. To the extent Meridian personnel create Client Deliverables, such personnel agree not to exercise moral rights in a manner that would interfere with Client's exercise of its licensed rights, including Client's rights to modify, adapt, and create derivative works of Client Deliverables as permitted under the applicable license tier. To the extent Act 55-2012 permits a partial written waiver of the right of integrity, Meridian waives the integrity right solely with respect to modifications of Client Deliverables made by Client in furtherance of Client's business operations.

Section 10.5 Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for failure to perform (except payment) due to circumstances beyond reasonable control. Notification within 48 hours. Mitigation efforts required. Either Party may terminate if force majeure continues beyond 90 days. Paid licenses and confidentiality obligations survive.

Section 10.6 Notices

Written notices required. Email sufficient for operational matters (disputes, approvals). Certified mail or courier required for termination and formal dispute resolution.

Section 10.7 Entire Agreement; Amendment

This Agreement, together with all executed SOWs and Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement. Amendments require written agreement signed by both Parties. No oral modification.

Section 10.8 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions continue in effect.

Section 10.9 No Waiver

Failure to enforce a provision does not waive the right to enforce it later.
