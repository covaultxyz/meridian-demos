Swag Meeting July 31

INTERNAL 2026-06-08 alex

Summary

The team discussed and defined the swag order fulfillment processes for stock, non-stock, and drop-ship orders, including warehouse workflows, SKU creation, unit conversions, and system configurations in D365 and BigCommerce.

Decisions Made

  • Swag outbound location will be changed from bulk storage to a shipping location ID.
  • Stock orders will use a pick-to-ship workflow instead of a pack station, eliminating the need for manager intervention.
  • Non-stock orders will be received into warehouse one, moved to a production tent for QC and photos, and then to swag outbound for shipping.
  • The accounting location will be phased out and eventually removed.
  • Unit conversions (e.g., per brick, case quantities) need to be built for BIC and Clipper products.
  • New stock and non-stock orders will be processed using the new workflow immediately.
  • Legacy orders will continue to be handled individually until converted.
  • Sales order status will be set to 'Awaiting Payment' by default, then moved to 'Awaiting Fulfillment' with a note specifying drop-ship, in-house, or overseas.

Commitments

  • Andrea will help coordinate and create missing SKUs with SS designations, picking up where Baca left off.
  • Jose and Danny will implement the new stock order workflow, with Danny toggling the swag outbound location and setting up the workflow.
  • Participants will update notes on BigCommerce to indicate when non-stock items are received and ready.
  • The meeting audio will be sent to Alex for AI overview extraction.
  • The meeting organizer will update the meeting email with action items.

Key Discoveries

  • Many SKUs needed for swag products (especially BIC and Clipper colors) are not yet created; the previous effort by Baca was extensive but incomplete.
  • The color-matching fee for Clippers is a $30 service SKU that can be added as a line item.
  • Unit conversions (e.g., per brick vs. per case) do not yet exist in D365, requiring manual setup.
  • The dropship module eliminates the need for a warehouse location for those transactions, as they are 100% digital.
  • There is no formal system to track labor and materials costs per order; it is currently expensed manually.
