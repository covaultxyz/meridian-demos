Call Prep System — Playbook Updates

_Lessons from the Garrett Taylor call applied to the system | 2026-03-27_

---

Problem Statement

The Garrett Taylor call prep was a mechanical success (polished cheatsheet, mock audio, investor deliverables, holdback strategy) built on an intelligence failure (wrong person profile). This document captures the systemic changes needed so this doesn't happen again.

---

Fix 1: Research Verification Gate

The Problem

The research pipeline produced a high-confidence profile for the wrong Garrett Taylor. There were no "unverified" flags, no confidence scores, and no human checkpoint before the prep was consumed.

The Fix

Add a mandatory verification step between research and prep generation.

Before any cheatsheet or mock call is generated, the research output must pass through:

1. **Cross-reference check**: Does the person the introducer (Michael) described match the research profile? Michael said "strong guy to play golf with, down to earth." The research described a Forbes-listed wealth management CEO. Even this vague mismatch should trigger a flag.

2. **Direct question to introducer**: "We're about to build prep materials. Here's what we found — does this match the Garrett you know?" One Telegram message. 30 seconds. Would have caught this.

3. **Confidence scoring on research output**: Every research profile should include:

• `confidence: high/medium/low`

• `verified_by: [source]` (LinkedIn confirmed, introducer confirmed, public records only)

• `potential_conflicts: [list]` (e.g., "Multiple Garrett Taylors in finance/e-commerce")

4. **Distinct person disambiguation**: When the research finds a common name, it should flag this and present multiple candidate profiles rather than collapsing into one.

Implementation

• Add a `verification_status` field to cheatsheet generation

• Block mock call audio generation until `verification_status: confirmed`

• Add "Introducer Verification" step to the call prep workflow

---

Fix 2: Call Time Boxing

The Problem

The pitch section ran 25 minutes (planned: 15). Three speakers with no conductor. Garrett's questions were delayed by 15 minutes.

The Fix

Hard time gates in the call strategy with designated speaker per section.

Update the cheatsheet template to include:

```

| Phase | Lead Speaker | Max Time | Hard Gate |

|-------|-------------|----------|-----------|

| Opening | Michael | 5 min | At 5 min: "Let's show you what we've built." |

| Pitch | Alexander | 10 min | At 10 min: "What questions does this raise?" |

| Their input | Let them talk | 15 min | Don't interrupt until they ask a question back |

| Close | Michael | 5 min | "What would be most useful for us to send you?" |

```

Rules:

• **One lead speaker per phase** — others contribute only when directly asked

• **Mandatory pause at the end of the pitch** — "What questions does this raise for you?"

• **Ask about their schedule in the first 2 minutes** — "How much time do you have today?"

• **If the pitch is running over 12 minutes**: Cut it. Say "I could go deeper, but I'd rather hear your reaction to what I've shared so far."

---

Fix 3: Adaptive Pitch Based on What You Hear

The Problem

The team heard Garrett say "I'm intentionally downsizing" at minute 10 and then spent 50 more minutes pitching solutions for scaling. The call plan was rigid — it didn't adapt to the information revealed in Phase 1.

The Fix

Build branching logic into the call strategy.

After Phase 1 ("Let them talk"), categorize what you heard:

| Signal | They said... | Adapt pitch to... |

|--------|-------------|-------------------|

| **Growth mode** | "Looking to scale," "hiring," "new market" | Efficiency + automation + scaling capacity |

| **Wind-down mode** | "Downsizing," "simplifying," "not looking to grow" | Knowledge monetization, passive income, advisory tools |

| **Pain mode** | "Struggling with X," "losing money on Y" | Direct solution to their specific pain |

| **Curiosity mode** | "Interesting," "tell me more" (no specific pain) | Quick demo, let the product speak, don't oversell |

| **Skeptic mode** | "I've seen a lot of AI," "what's different" | Differentiation, live proof, avoid hype |

Garrett was clearly in **wind-down mode** + **curiosity mode**. The pitch should have been:

• 5-minute demo (show, don't tell)

• "Your expertise is the asset. We turn it into a product."

• "What would you want to do if you didn't have to run the agency?"

Instead, the team ran the growth-mode pitch for 25 minutes.

Implementation

• Add a "Branching Logic" section to every cheatsheet

• After Phase 1, the call leader takes 30 seconds to mentally classify which mode the prospect is in

• Cheatsheet includes 3-4 pre-built pitch variants (1 paragraph each) for the most likely modes

Fix 4: Demo-First for Non-Technical Prospects

The Problem

Garrett isn't technical. He uses ChatGPT and Claude casually. The call was 100% verbal description of a technical system — no visuals, no demo, no screen share.

The Fix

Default to showing a demo within the first 10 minutes for any non-technical prospect.

Pre-call checklist addition:

• [ ] Is this prospect technical? (If no → demo within 10 minutes)

• [ ] Is the demo environment pre-flighted? (Dashboard, Telegram, NATS, MIG)

• [ ] Is screen sharing set up?

The demo doesn't need to be the full 5-act script. A 3-minute "watch this" moment:

1. Send a message in Telegram

2. Agent responds with intelligence

3. "That just queried 17,900 memory nodes and coordinated across 3 servers in 4 seconds."

Three minutes. More convincing than 25 minutes of description.

Fix 5: Post-Call Action Protocol

The Problem

The pre-call TODO document included "After the Call" actions (update cheatsheet, store MIG intelligence, execute deliverables). These haven't been executed yet.

The Fix

Automate the post-call workflow trigger.

When a meeting transcript lands in `data/meetings/`, the system should:

1. **Auto-generate** a corrected profile (compare research vs. transcript)

2. **Flag mismatches** between prep and reality

3. **Prompt for MIG storage** of key intelligence

4. **Update the cheatsheet** with a "Last Meeting Recap" section

5. **Create follow-up task** with deadline

Implementation

This could be a sentinel or a manual runbook. Start with the manual version:

```

Post-Call Checklist:

[ ] Compare transcript to research profile — flag mismatches

[ ] Store corrected profile in MIG (mig_remember, importance 7-8)

[ ] Update cheatsheet HTML with "Last Meeting Recap"

[ ] Create follow-up action items with owners and deadlines

[ ] Grade the call (use 01-*-grade-report.md as template)

```

Fix 6: Speaker Discipline Rules

The Problem

Three speakers pitched simultaneously, creating overlap, repetition, and no clear narrative arc.

The Fix

Establish speaker protocol before every prospect call.

Pre-call alignment (2 minutes in the waiting room):

1. **Who leads each section?** Assign by name.

2. **Who's on backup?** Others listen and take notes. Jump in ONLY if directly asked or if they have a specific 30-second point.

3. **What's the cutoff signal?** If someone is running long, the designated timekeeper sends a Telegram message or taps the table.

4. **Who closes?** Same person who opened. Bookend creates coherence.

For the Meridian team specifically:

• **Michael**: Opens calls (rapport), closes calls (next steps), handles business questions

• **Alexander**: Demos the product, answers "how does it work" questions

• **Ely**: Answers architecture/technical depth questions (only when asked)

• **Default**: If Garrett asks a general question, Michael fields it. If technical, defer to Alexander. If deep-technical, defer to Ely.

Fix 7: "Read the Room" Signals Checklist

The Problem

Garrett said "not really" to the agency use case and the team asked twice more.

The Fix

Add explicit "stop pushing" signals to every cheatsheet.

| Signal | They said... | What to do |

|--------|-------------|------------|

| "Not really" | Direct rejection | Pivot immediately. Do NOT rephrase the same pitch. |

| "I need to think about it" | Soft maybe | Accept. Move on. Don't push for commitment. |

| "That's interesting" (flat tone) | Polite disengagement | Ask: "What part resonates? What doesn't?" |

| "I don't know if my [X] is the right fit" | They're telling you it's not | Say: "Makes sense. What would be the right fit?" |

| Changes subject | Lost interest | Follow their lead. Come back to your point later, or don't. |

| Looks at phone/time | Ready to leave | Compress to closing. Don't start new topics. |

**Three-strike rule**: If the prospect declines the same angle twice, it's dead. Do NOT attempt a third time. Pivot to a completely different value proposition.
