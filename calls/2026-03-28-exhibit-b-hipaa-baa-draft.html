HIPAA / HITECH Compliance Addendum

*Pursuant to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act, and applicable regulations at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164*

---

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

**This Business Associate Agreement** ("BAA") is entered into by and between:

Covered Entity:

Millennial Behavioral Management, LLC ("MBM"), a Puerto Rico limited liability company operating under Act 60, doing business through Ayala Child, Adolescent & Adult Psychiatry, PC, a New Jersey Professional Corporation (collectively, "Covered Entity")

Business Associate:

[CONTRACTOR NAME / ENTITY] ("Business Associate" or "Contractor")

This BAA is incorporated as Exhibit B to that certain Intellectual Property Agreement (the "Underlying Agreement") entered into by and between MBM and Business Associate on [DATE], and governs the obligations of Business Associate with respect to Protected Health Information accessed, created, received, maintained, or transmitted in connection with the development, deployment, and maintenance of the LAN-based clinical and administrative psychiatric platform with cloud/API backup capabilities ("Clinical Platform" or "Platform").

ARTICLE I — RECITALS AND REGULATORY BACKGROUND

**1.1** Covered Entity is a covered entity as defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), Public Law 104-191, and the regulations promulgated thereunder at 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164, as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act"), Title XIII of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009, Public Law 111-5.

**1.2** Business Associate performs functions and activities on behalf of Covered Entity involving the use and/or disclosure of Protected Health Information, including the design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance of the Clinical Platform, which creates, receives, maintains, and transmits electronic Protected Health Information.

**1.3** The Parties acknowledge that HIPAA is federal law applicable in all United States jurisdictions, including the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, and that Business Associate must comply with the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E), the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C), and the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D), as directly applicable to business associates under the HITECH Act.

**1.4** The Parties further acknowledge that the Clinical Platform will process psychiatric and behavioral health data, including information subject to heightened protections under:

• (a) HIPAA Psychotherapy Notes provisions (45 CFR 164.501, 164.508(a)(2));

• (b) 42 CFR Part 2 — Confidentiality of Substance Use Disorder Patient Records, as amended by the Final Rule effective April 16, 2024, with a compliance date of February 16, 2026;

• (c) The New Jersey Data Privacy Act (NJDPA) as amended January 2026; and

• (d) Puerto Rico Personal Data Protection Act (Act No. 81-2019).

**1.5** While the Underlying Agreement is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, this BAA is governed by and shall be interpreted in accordance with federal HIPAA and HITECH requirements, which preempt contrary provisions of state and territorial law except where state or territorial law provides more stringent privacy protections. Where applicable state or territorial law (including New Jersey and Puerto Rico law) imposes requirements more protective of individual privacy than HIPAA, Business Associate shall comply with the more protective standard. *See* 45 CFR 160.203.

ARTICLE II — DEFINITIONS

**2.1** Unless otherwise defined herein, all capitalized terms shall have the meanings set forth in 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164. The following terms are specifically defined for purposes of this BAA:

**(a) "Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of Protected Health Information in a manner not permitted under Subpart E of 45 CFR Part 164 which compromises the security or privacy of the Protected Health Information, as defined at 45 CFR 164.402, subject to the exclusions set forth therein.

**(b) "Designated Record Set"** means a group of records maintained by or for a Covered Entity that comprises the medical and billing records about Individuals maintained by or for a Covered Entity; enrollment, payment, claims adjudication, and case or medical management record systems maintained by or for a health plan; or records used, in whole or in part, by or for the Covered Entity to make decisions about Individuals. *See* 45 CFR 164.501.

**(c) "Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means Protected Health Information that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media, as defined at 45 CFR 160.103.

**(d) "Individual"** means the person who is the subject of the Protected Health Information and includes a person who qualifies as a personal representative in accordance with 45 CFR 164.502(g).

**(e) "Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means individually identifiable health information transmitted by electronic media, maintained in electronic media, or transmitted or maintained in any other form or medium, as defined at 45 CFR 160.103. For purposes of this BAA, PHI includes all categories of patient data processed by the Clinical Platform, including but not limited to:

• Patient demographics and identifiers

• Clinical assessment data

• Treatment plans and progress notes

• Psychotherapy notes (as separately defined)

• Medication records

• Billing and insurance information

• Scheduling and appointment data

• Any substance use disorder (SUD) treatment records

**(f) "Psychotherapy Notes"** means notes recorded (in any medium) by a health care provider who is a mental health professional documenting or analyzing the contents of conversation during a private counseling session or a group, joint, or family counseling session, and that are separated from the rest of the Individual's medical record. Psychotherapy Notes excludes medication prescription and monitoring, counseling session start and stop times, the modalities and frequencies of treatment furnished, results of clinical tests, and any summary of diagnosis, functional status, the treatment plan, symptoms, prognosis, and progress to date. *See* 45 CFR 164.501.

**(g) "Required by Law"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR 164.103.

**(h) "Secretary"** means the Secretary of the United States Department of Health and Human Services ("HHS") or any designee thereof.

**(i) "Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system, as defined at 45 CFR 164.304.

**(j) "SUD Records"** means records of the identity, diagnosis, prognosis, or treatment of any patient which are maintained in connection with the performance of any federally assisted program or activity relating to substance use disorder education, prevention, training, treatment, rehabilitation, or research, and which are subject to the protections of 42 CFR Part 2.

**(k) "Unsecured Protected Health Information"** means Protected Health Information that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized persons through the use of a technology or methodology specified by the Secretary in guidance issued under 42 U.S.C. 17932(h)(2). *See* 45 CFR 164.402.

ARTICLE III — PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

*[45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(i); 164.504(e)(2)(ii)]*

**3.1 Permitted Uses.** Business Associate may use or disclose PHI solely:

**(a)** As necessary to perform functions, activities, or services for, or on behalf of, Covered Entity as specified in the Underlying Agreement, including the design, development, testing, deployment, support, and maintenance of the Clinical Platform;

**(b)** As Required by Law;

**(c)** For the proper management and administration of Business Associate, provided that such uses are necessary for the proper management and administration of Business Associate and provided that disclosures are Required by Law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is disclosed that the information will remain confidential and will be used or further disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purposes for which it was disclosed, and the person notifies Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been breached; *[45 CFR 164.504(e)(4)]*

**(d)** To provide Data Aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by 45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B), if applicable.

**3.2 Prohibited Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate shall NOT:

**(a)** Use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this BAA or as Required by Law; *[45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(A)]*

**(b)** Use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate Subpart E of 45 CFR Part 164 if done by Covered Entity, except as permitted under Sections 3.1(c) and 3.1(d) above; *[45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(B)]*

**(c)** Use or disclose PHI for marketing purposes or receive remuneration in exchange for PHI, except with Covered Entity's prior written authorization and in compliance with 45 CFR 164.508(a)(3) and (4);

**(d)** Sell PHI as defined under 45 CFR 164.502(a)(5)(ii);

**(e)** Use or disclose Psychotherapy Notes for any purpose other than as specifically authorized in writing by the Individual whose notes are at issue, or as permitted under the limited exceptions at 45 CFR 164.508(a)(2)(ii), and only to the extent necessary for the technical function being performed;

**(f)** Use or disclose SUD Records except as permitted by the patient's written consent or as otherwise permitted by 42 CFR Part 2, as amended, and shall not redisclose SUD Records in violation of the Part 2 prohibition on redisclosure;

**(g)** Use or disclose PHI for the purpose of training artificial intelligence or machine learning models, whether internal or third-party, without the prior express written consent of Covered Entity and each affected Individual;

**(h)** Aggregate, de-identify, or re-identify PHI except as expressly authorized by Covered Entity in writing and in compliance with the de-identification standards at 45 CFR 164.514.

**3.3 Minimum Necessary Standard.** Business Associate shall, in its performance of functions and activities under this BAA, make reasonable efforts to use, disclose, and request only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request. *[45 CFR 164.502(b); 164.514(d)]* For software development purposes, this means:

**(a)** Development and testing environments shall use de-identified data or synthetic data wherever feasible;

**(b)** Where access to PHI is required for debugging, troubleshooting, or data migration, access shall be limited to the specific records necessary and logged;

**(c)** Business Associate personnel shall access only the data elements required by their specific function and shall not browse or access PHI outside the scope of a specific task.

ARTICLE IV — SAFEGUARDS

Section 4.1 — General Safeguard Obligation

Business Associate shall use appropriate safeguards, and comply with Subpart C of 45 CFR Part 164 (the Security Rule) with respect to ePHI, to prevent the use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided for by this BAA. *[45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(B); 164.314(a)(2)(i)]*

Section 4.2 — Administrative Safeguards *[45 CFR 164.308]*

Business Associate shall implement the following administrative safeguards:

**(a) Security Management Process** *[164.308(a)(1)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations, including:

• (i) A comprehensive and accurate risk analysis identifying potential risks and vulnerabilities to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI held by or on behalf of Business Associate *[164.308(a)(1)(ii)(A)]*;

• (ii) Risk management measures sufficient to reduce risks and vulnerabilities to a reasonable and appropriate level *[164.308(a)(1)(ii)(B)]*;

• (iii) A sanction policy for workforce members who fail to comply with security policies *[164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C)]*;

• (iv) Regular review of information system activity records (audit logs, access reports, security incident tracking) *[164.308(a)(1)(ii)(D)]*.

**(b) Assigned Security Responsibility** *[164.308(a)(2)]*: Business Associate shall identify a security official responsible for the development and implementation of its security policies and procedures.

**(c) Workforce Security** *[164.308(a)(3)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to ensure that all members of its workforce have appropriate access to ePHI and to prevent workforce members who should not have access from obtaining access, including:

• (i) Authorization and/or supervision of workforce members who work with ePHI *[164.308(a)(3)(ii)(A)]*;

• (ii) Procedures to determine that the access of a workforce member to ePHI is appropriate *[164.308(a)(3)(ii)(B)]*;

• (iii) Procedures for terminating access to ePHI when the employment of, or other arrangement with, a workforce member ends or when access is no longer necessary *[164.308(a)(3)(ii)(C)]*.

**(d) Information Access Management** *[164.308(a)(4)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures for authorizing access to ePHI, including role-based access controls aligned with the principle of least privilege.

**(e) Security Awareness and Training** *[164.308(a)(5)]*: Business Associate shall implement a security awareness and training program for all workforce members who access PHI, including training on:

• (i) Recognizing and reporting security reminders and threats;

• (ii) Protection from malicious software;

• (iii) Log-in monitoring;

• (iv) Password management.

**(f) Security Incident Procedures** *[164.308(a)(6)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to address Security Incidents, including identification, response, mitigation, and documentation.

**(g) Contingency Plan** *[164.308(a)(7)]*: Business Associate shall establish and implement a contingency plan for responding to an emergency or other occurrence that damages systems containing ePHI, including:

• (i) Data backup plan *[164.308(a)(7)(ii)(A)]*;

• (ii) Disaster recovery plan *[164.308(a)(7)(ii)(B)]*;

• (iii) Emergency mode operation plan *[164.308(a)(7)(ii)(C)]*;

• (iv) Testing and revision procedures *[164.308(a)(7)(ii)(D)]*;

• (v) Applications and data criticality analysis *[164.308(a)(7)(ii)(E)]*.

**(h) Evaluation** *[164.308(a)(8)]*: Business Associate shall perform periodic technical and non-technical evaluations to assess whether its security policies and procedures meet the requirements of the Security Rule.

Section 4.3 — Physical Safeguards *[45 CFR 164.310]*

Business Associate shall implement the following physical safeguards:

**(a) Facility Access Controls** *[164.310(a)]*: Business Associate shall limit physical access to its electronic information systems and the facilities in which they are housed, while ensuring that properly authorized access is allowed, including:

• (i) Contingency operations procedures *[164.310(a)(2)(i)]*;

• (ii) A facility security plan *[164.310(a)(2)(ii)]*;

• (iii) Access control and validation procedures *[164.310(a)(2)(iii)]*;

• (iv) Maintenance records *[164.310(a)(2)(iv)]*.

**(b) Workstation Use** *[164.310(b)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies specifying the proper functions to be performed and the physical attributes of the surroundings of workstations that access ePHI. All workstations used for Clinical Platform development shall:

• (i) Be protected by full-disk encryption;

• (ii) Require authentication to unlock;

• (iii) Be configured with automatic screen lock after a period of inactivity not to exceed 15 minutes.

**(c) Workstation Security** *[164.310(c)]*: Business Associate shall implement physical safeguards for all workstations that access ePHI to restrict access to authorized users.

**(d) Device and Media Controls** *[164.310(d)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies governing the receipt, removal, and disposal of hardware and electronic media containing ePHI, including:

• (i) Disposal — policies for final disposition of ePHI and/or the hardware or electronic media on which it is stored, using NIST SP 800-88 compliant methods *[164.310(d)(2)(i)]*;

• (ii) Media re-use — procedures for removal of ePHI from electronic media before re-use *[164.310(d)(2)(ii)]*;

• (iii) Accountability — records of movements of hardware and electronic media and the persons responsible *[164.310(d)(2)(iii)]*;

• (iv) Data backup and storage — creation of a retrievable, exact copy of ePHI before movement of equipment *[164.310(d)(2)(iv)]*.

Section 4.4 — Technical Safeguards *[45 CFR 164.312]*

Business Associate shall implement the following technical safeguards:

**(a) Access Controls** *[164.312(a)]*: Business Associate shall implement technical policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI to allow access only to those persons or software programs that have been granted access rights, including:

• (i) **Unique user identification** (Required) — assign a unique name and/or number for identifying and tracking user identity *[164.312(a)(2)(i)]*;

• (ii) **Emergency access procedure** (Required) — establish and implement procedures for obtaining necessary ePHI during an emergency *[164.312(a)(2)(ii)]*;

• (iii) **Automatic logoff** (Addressable) — implement electronic procedures that terminate an electronic session after a predetermined time of inactivity *[164.312(a)(2)(iii)]*;

• (iv) **Encryption and decryption** (Addressable, but see Section 4.5) — implement a mechanism to encrypt and decrypt ePHI *[164.312(a)(2)(iv)]*.

**(b) Audit Controls** *[164.312(b)]* (Required): Business Associate shall implement hardware, software, and/or procedural mechanisms that record and examine activity in information systems that contain or use ePHI. Audit logs shall:

• (i) Record user identification, date, time, and nature of each access to ePHI;

• (ii) Be retained for a minimum of six (6) years;

• (iii) Be reviewed regularly and upon any suspected Security Incident;

• (iv) Be made available to Covered Entity upon request within ten (10) business days.

**(c) Integrity Controls** *[164.312(c)]*: Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction, including electronic mechanisms to corroborate that ePHI has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner *[164.312(c)(2)]*.

**(d) Person or Entity Authentication** *[164.312(d)]* (Required): Business Associate shall implement procedures to verify that a person or entity seeking access to ePHI is who or what it claims to be. Multi-factor authentication ("MFA") shall be required for:

• (i) All remote access to systems containing ePHI;

• (ii) All privileged/administrative access;

• (iii) All access to production environments containing PHI.

**(e) Transmission Security** *[164.312(e)]*: Business Associate shall implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI that is being transmitted over an electronic communications network, including:

• (i) **Integrity controls** — mechanisms to ensure that electronically transmitted ePHI is not improperly modified without detection *[164.312(e)(2)(i)]*;

• (ii) **Encryption** — mechanisms to encrypt ePHI whenever it is transmitted over open or public networks *[164.312(e)(2)(ii)]*.

Section 4.5 — Encryption Requirements (Enhanced)

Given the nature of the Clinical Platform and the sensitivity of psychiatric and behavioral health data:

**(a)** Encryption of ePHI at rest is REQUIRED, not addressable, under this BAA. All databases, file systems, backups, and storage media containing ePHI shall be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent.

**(b)** Encryption of ePHI in transit is REQUIRED. All network transmissions of ePHI shall use TLS 1.2 or higher, or equivalent encryption.

**(c)** For the LAN-based Clinical Platform:

• (i) All database connections transmitting ePHI shall use encrypted channels (TLS/SSL);

• (ii) Any cloud/API backup functionality shall encrypt data both in transit and at rest;

• (iii) Local network segments carrying ePHI should be segmented from general network traffic where feasible;

• (iv) Wireless network access to ePHI, if any, shall require WPA3 or equivalent encryption.

**(d)** This enhanced encryption requirement reflects:

• (i) New Jersey's mandatory encryption law for devices containing PHI;

• (ii) The HHS NPRM published January 6, 2025 (RIN 0945-AA22) proposing to make encryption of ePHI at rest and in transit a required (non-addressable) specification;

• (iii) OCR enforcement trends emphasizing encryption failures as a primary enforcement target.

Section 4.6 — Policies and Procedures; Documentation *[45 CFR 164.316]*

**(a)** Business Associate shall implement reasonable and appropriate policies and procedures to comply with the Security Rule standards and implementation specifications.

**(b)** Business Associate shall maintain written (which may be electronic) documentation of policies, procedures, actions, activities, and assessments required by the Security Rule.

**(c)** Documentation shall be retained for six (6) years from the date of its creation or the date when it last was in effect, whichever is later. *[45 CFR 164.316(b)(2)(i)]*

**(d)** Documentation shall be made available to those persons responsible for implementing the procedures to which the documentation pertains and shall be reviewed and updated periodically. *[45 CFR 164.316(b)(2)(ii)-(iii)]*

ARTICLE V — BREACH NOTIFICATION

*[45 CFR 164.410; 164.402]*

Section 5.1 — Notification Obligation

**(a)** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Breach of Unsecured Protected Health Information without unreasonable delay and in no case later than **thirty (30) calendar days** after discovery of such Breach. *[Note: 45 CFR 164.410(a) permits up to 60 days; this BAA contractually accelerates the timeline to 30 days given the sensitivity of psychiatric data.]*

**(b)** A Breach shall be treated as discovered by Business Associate as of the first day on which such Breach is known to Business Associate, or by exercising reasonable diligence would have been known to Business Associate. Business Associate shall be deemed to have knowledge of a Breach if the Breach is known, or by exercising reasonable diligence would have been known, to any person, other than the person committing the Breach, who is an employee, officer, or other agent of Business Associate. *[45 CFR 164.410(a)(2)]*

Section 5.2 — Content of Notification

Business Associate's notification to Covered Entity shall include, to the extent possible:

**(a)** The identification of each Individual whose Unsecured Protected Health Information has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed during the Breach; *[45 CFR 164.410(c)(1)]*

**(b)** A description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery of the Breach, if known; *[45 CFR 164.404(c)(1)(A)]*

**(c)** A description of the types of Unsecured PHI that were involved in the Breach (such as full name, Social Security number, date of birth, home address, account number, diagnosis, disability code, or similar types of information); *[45 CFR 164.404(c)(1)(B)]*

**(d)** Any steps Individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm resulting from the Breach; *[45 CFR 164.404(c)(1)(C)]*

**(e)** A description of what Business Associate is doing to investigate the Breach, mitigate harm to Individuals, and protect against any further Breaches; *[45 CFR 164.404(c)(1)(D)]*

**(f)** Contact procedures, including a toll-free telephone number, email address, website, or postal address, at which affected Individuals (through Covered Entity) may obtain additional information; *[45 CFR 164.404(c)(1)(E)]*

**(g)** Whether the Breach involved Psychotherapy Notes or SUD Records subject to 42 CFR Part 2; and

**(h)** The results of Business Associate's risk assessment under 45 CFR 164.402(2), analyzing the probability that the PHI has been compromised based on:

• (i) The nature and extent of the PHI involved, including the types of identifiers and the likelihood of re-identification;

• (ii) The unauthorized person who used the PHI or to whom the disclosure was made;

• (iii) Whether the PHI was actually acquired or viewed;

• (iv) The extent to which the risk to the PHI has been mitigated.

Section 5.3 — Notification of Security Incidents

**(a)** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Security Incident of which it becomes aware. *[45 CFR 164.314(a)(2)(i)(C)]*

**(b)** Successful Security Incidents (unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI, or interference with system operations) shall be reported within seventy-two (72) hours of discovery.

**(c)** Attempted Security Incidents (unsuccessful attempts, such as pings, port scans, or failed log-in attempts) shall be reported in aggregate in a monthly written summary, unless a pattern suggests a targeted attack, in which case immediate reporting is required.

Section 5.4 — Cooperation in Breach Response

**(a)** Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity in investigating the Breach and in fulfilling Covered Entity's obligations to notify affected Individuals, the Secretary, and where required, the media. *[45 CFR 164.404; 164.406; 164.408]*

**(b)** Business Associate shall preserve all evidence related to the Breach for at least six (6) years and shall provide Covered Entity, and any regulatory authority or law enforcement agency authorized by Covered Entity, with full access to relevant records, systems, and personnel.

**(c)** Covered Entity shall be responsible for all notifications to Individuals, HHS, and media unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing. Business Associate shall bear the reasonable costs of notification and credit monitoring services to the extent that the Breach was caused by Business Associate's acts or omissions.

Section 5.5 — SUD Records Breach

For Breaches involving SUD Records subject to 42 CFR Part 2, the Parties acknowledge that the 2024 Final Rule (effective February 16, 2026) aligns Part 2 breach notification with HIPAA breach notification requirements. Business Associate shall comply with both the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule and any additional requirements of 42 CFR Part 2, and shall clearly identify SUD Records in its Breach notifications to enable Covered Entity to fulfill any additional Part 2 obligations.

ARTICLE VI — SUBCONTRACTORS

*[45 CFR 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(D); 164.502(e)(1)(ii)]*

Section 6.1 — Subcontractor BAA Requirement

**(a)** Business Associate shall ensure that any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate agrees to the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements that apply to Business Associate under this BAA with respect to such PHI.

**(b)** Business Associate shall enter into a written agreement with each Subcontractor that complies with 45 CFR 164.504(e) and 164.314(a) before permitting the Subcontractor to access any PHI.

**(c)** Business Associate shall not engage any Subcontractor to perform services involving PHI without the prior written consent of Covered Entity.

Section 6.2 — Subcontractor Oversight

**(a)** Business Associate shall maintain a current, written list of all Subcontractors who have access to PHI and shall provide such list to Covered Entity upon request.

**(b)** Business Associate shall periodically evaluate whether its Subcontractors are in compliance with the terms of their subcontractor agreements and the HIPAA Rules.

**(c)** Business Associate shall remain fully liable for any Breach, failure, or non-compliance by its Subcontractors as if such Breach, failure, or non-compliance were committed by Business Associate itself.

Section 6.3 — Cloud Service Providers and API Services

Given that the Clinical Platform includes cloud/API backup capabilities:

**(a)** Any cloud hosting, storage, or infrastructure-as-a-service provider that will access, store, transmit, or process ePHI in connection with the Platform is a Subcontractor for purposes of this BAA and must execute a BAA complying with Section 6.1.

**(b)** Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with documentation of the cloud provider's BAA, security certifications (SOC 2 Type II, HITRUST, or equivalent), and a summary of the provider's security practices before migrating any PHI to the cloud environment.

**(c)** Business Associate shall ensure that all cloud/API backup data is stored within the United States (including its territories) and is encrypted at rest and in transit as required by Section 4.5.

ARTICLE VII — INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS

Section 7.1 — Access to PHI *[45 CFR 164.524]*

**(a)** Business Associate shall, within ten (10) business days of a request from Covered Entity, make available to Covered Entity any PHI in a Designated Record Set maintained by Business Associate, in order for Covered Entity to fulfill its obligations under 45 CFR 164.524.

**(b)** If an Individual makes a request for access directly to Business Associate, Business Associate shall forward such request to Covered Entity within five (5) business days.

**(c)** Business Associate shall maintain ePHI in the Designated Record Set in an electronic format and shall provide such ePHI in the electronic form and format requested by the Individual (through Covered Entity), if it is readily producible in such form and format, or, if not, in a readable electronic form and format as agreed to by Covered Entity and the Individual. *[45 CFR 164.524(c)(2)(ii)]*

Section 7.2 — Amendment of PHI *[45 CFR 164.526]*

**(a)** Business Associate shall, within ten (10) business days of receiving a request from Covered Entity, make PHI available for amendment and incorporate any amendments to PHI in a Designated Record Set as directed by Covered Entity.

**(b)** Business Associate shall not independently amend PHI in the Designated Record Set.

Section 7.3 — Accounting of Disclosures *[45 CFR 164.528]*

**(a)** Business Associate shall maintain a log of all disclosures of PHI made by Business Associate as would be required for Covered Entity to respond to a request by an Individual for an accounting of disclosures in accordance with 45 CFR 164.528.

**(b)** Such log shall include, for each disclosure:

• (i) The date of the disclosure;

• (ii) The name of the entity or person who received the PHI and, if known, the address of such entity or person;

• (iii) A brief description of the PHI disclosed; and

• (iv) A brief statement of the purpose of the disclosure.

**(c)** Business Associate shall make such log available to Covered Entity within ten (10) business days of Covered Entity's request.

**(d)** Business Associate shall retain such log for at least six (6) years from the date of the last disclosure recorded.

Section 7.4 — Restrictions on Disclosures *[45 CFR 164.522]*

**(a)** Business Associate shall comply with any restrictions on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to or is required to abide by under 45 CFR 164.522, to the extent that such restrictions affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI, provided that Covered Entity has notified Business Associate of such restrictions in writing.

ARTICLE VIII — SPECIAL PROTECTIONS FOR PSYCHIATRIC AND BEHAVIORAL HEALTH DATA

Section 8.1 — Psychotherapy Notes

**(a)** Business Associate acknowledges that Psychotherapy Notes, as defined in Section 2.1(f) and 45 CFR 164.501, receive heightened protection under HIPAA.

**(b)** The Clinical Platform shall be designed to maintain Psychotherapy Notes separate from the rest of the Individual's medical record, consistent with 45 CFR 164.501.

**(c)** Business Associate shall not use or disclose Psychotherapy Notes except:

• (i) As authorized in writing by the Individual;

• (ii) For use by the originator of the Psychotherapy Notes for treatment;

• (iii) For Covered Entity's own training programs in which students, trainees, or practitioners in mental health learn under supervision to practice or improve their skills;

• (iv) To defend Covered Entity or Business Associate in a legal action or other proceeding brought by the Individual; or

• (v) As Required by Law, including for oversight of the originator of the notes, for law enforcement purposes regarding the death of the Individual, to avert a serious threat to health or safety, or for lawful activities of coroners and medical examiners.

*[45 CFR 164.508(a)(2)]*

**(d)** The Platform shall implement technical controls that enforce the separation and restricted-access requirements for Psychotherapy Notes, including distinct access controls and audit logging for notes separate from general medical records.

Section 8.2 — Substance Use Disorder Records (42 CFR Part 2)

**(a)** To the extent that the Clinical Platform creates, receives, maintains, or transmits SUD Records, Business Associate shall comply with all applicable provisions of 42 CFR Part 2, as amended by the February 2024 Final Rule (compliance date February 16, 2026).

**(b)** SUD Records shall be subject to the following additional protections:

• (i) **Consent for Disclosure**: SUD Records may only be disclosed with the patient's prior written consent, except as specifically permitted by 42 CFR Part 2 (e.g., medical emergencies under 42 CFR 2.51, audit and evaluation under 42 CFR 2.53, or court orders meeting the criteria of 42 CFR 2.64-2.67).

• (ii) **Prohibition on Redisclosure**: Any person who receives SUD Records from Business Associate is prohibited from making any further disclosure of the records without the specific written consent of the patient or as otherwise permitted by 42 CFR Part 2. Business Associate shall include the following notice (or its substantial equivalent) with any permissible disclosure of SUD Records:

> *"This record which has been disclosed to you is protected by federal confidentiality rules (42 CFR Part 2). The federal rules prohibit you from making any further disclosure of information in this record that identifies a patient as having or having had a substance use disorder either directly, by reference to publicly available information, or through verification of such identification by another person, unless further disclosure is expressly permitted by the written consent of the individual whose information is being disclosed or as otherwise permitted by 42 CFR Part 2. A general authorization for the release of medical or other information is NOT sufficient for this purpose (see 42 CFR 2.31). The federal rules restrict any use of the information to investigate or prosecute with regard to a crime any patient with a substance use disorder, except as provided at 42 CFR 2.12(c)(5) and 2.65."*

• (iii) **Alignment with HIPAA**: Consistent with the 2024 Final Rule, once a covered entity or business associate receives SUD Records pursuant to a valid Part 2 consent, the records may be used and disclosed for treatment, payment, and health care operations purposes in accordance with HIPAA, subject to the Part 2 prohibition on use in criminal proceedings without the patient's consent or a court order meeting the Part 2 criteria.

• (iv) **SUD Counseling Notes**: The 2024 Final Rule created a new category of "SUD counseling notes" analogous to Psychotherapy Notes, which require specific patient consent and cannot be used or disclosed based on a broad TPO consent. The Clinical Platform shall implement appropriate separation and access controls for SUD counseling notes.

**(c)** Business Associate shall assist Covered Entity in providing patients with an accounting of disclosures of SUD Records, consistent with the patient rights provisions of 42 CFR Part 2 as amended.

APPENDIX C — HHS/OCR ENFORCEMENT CONTEXT (2024-2026)

*This appendix provides regulatory context informing the security requirements of this BAA. It is for informational purposes and does not limit the obligations set forth in the BAA.*

Recent Enforcement Actions Against Software Business Associates

1. **MMG Fusion, LLC (March 2026)** — Software company serving as BA; impermissible disclosure of PHI of approximately 15 million individuals; failure to conduct adequate risk analysis; failure to notify covered entities of breach. Settlement: $10,000 plus 3-year corrective action plan monitored by OCR.

2. **Massachusetts EHR/Billing BA (January 2025)** — Cloud-based EHR and billing support services company; ransomware attack exposing ePHI of 31,248 patients. Settlement: $80,000. Cited: inadequate risk analysis, insufficient technical safeguards.

3. **Virginia Hosting/Cloud BA (January 2025)** — Data hosting and cloud services company; ransomware attack encrypting ePHI of twelve covered entities. Settlement: $90,000. Cited: inadequate risk analysis, lack of encryption.

Key Enforcement Trends

• **Risk Analysis Initiative**: In late 2024, OCR launched a dedicated Risk Analysis Initiative. Inadequate risk analysis was cited in 13 enforcement actions within the first six months. This is the single most frequently cited HIPAA violation.

• **Software and Cloud Vendors as Direct Targets**: OCR is increasingly pursuing enforcement directly against business associates (not just covered entities), particularly software vendors and cloud service providers.

• **Proposed Security Rule Strengthening**: The January 2025 NPRM proposes eliminating the "addressable" vs. "required" distinction for security specifications, making encryption, MFA, vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, and network segmentation mandatory for all regulated entities. Final rule expected late 2025 or 2026.

• **42 CFR Part 2 Alignment**: The February 2024 Final Rule (compliance date February 16, 2026) brings SUD records under HIPAA-like enforcement, including civil and criminal penalties replacing the prior criminal-only penalty structure.

Implications for This BAA

The security requirements in this BAA are calibrated to satisfy both current HIPAA requirements and reasonably anticipated near-term requirements under the proposed Security Rule, ensuring that Business Associate's compliance posture is forward-looking rather than reactive.
