Summary

Meridian/RankOnAmazon terminal presented a proposal to build an AI-augmented Amazon marketplace intelligence system for a Nordic agency, covering system architecture, pricing options, and partnership structure.

Decisions Made

  • Option B (joint venture partnership model) selected over Option A (full commercial rate)
  • Pilot will focus on the newly onboarded 40,000 SKU horse equipment client rather than spreading across multiple accounts
  • System will be built iteratively in batches starting with backend attribute engine and listing copy engine as foundational stations

Commitments

  • Carl to discuss Option B terms with shareholders and other partners (Sander mentioned) before finalizing
  • Carl/agency to send back their full wish list of additional service areas (ads/PPC, account health, etc.) so Meridian can map out additional stations and pricing
  • Michael to send the presentation deck to the agency team
  • Jennifer to download the category listing report for the 40k SKU client and begin analysis of top sellers this week
  • Agency team to get back to Meridian quickly with internal feedback and any revision requests

Key Discoveries

  • The 40,000 SKU horse equipment client has virtually no backend attributes populated, meaning quick wins are likely just from fixing that alone
  • The agency already has a profit-sharing arrangement with the 40k SKU client (20-33% of profit increases), making Meridian's backend optimization directly and immediately profitable for both parties
  • Fjällräven (billion-dollar outdoor brand) was just landed as a client starting with UK as proof of concept, with potential to expand globally
  • The Nordic Amazon agency market contracted sharply due to inflation, going from ~12 agencies to roughly 2-3, creating a window to dominate the market
  • The system is designed so that if the agency is acquired, all compounded intelligence and workflows transfer seamlessly to the acquirer
