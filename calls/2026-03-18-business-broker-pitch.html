---

The Thesis

Business owners preparing to sell face a paradox: **the things that would make their business most valuable are the things they've never documented.**

The buyer wants:

• Documented SOPs for every process

• Clean, organized financial data with clear reporting

• Systems that run without the owner

• Evidence that the business can scale

• Predictable, recurring processes (not tribal knowledge)

The seller has:

• 15-30 years of experience in their head

• Processes that "just work" because specific people know the steps

• Financial data spread across QuickBooks, spreadsheets, emails, and memory

• A business that would collapse without them for 2 weeks

**Meridian bridges this gap** — and in doing so, increases the sale multiple.

The Value Proposition for Nate

What You Tell the Business Broker

"You have 100 companies looking to sell. Half of them aren't ready — their processes aren't documented, their operations depend on the owner, and buyers see that as risk. We come in for 3-6 months, document everything, build operational intelligence into the business, and hand them back a company that's worth 20-50% more at sale. You get a higher commission. They get a higher multiple. We get paid on performance."

What We Actually Do

Phase 1: Assessment (Week 1-2)

• **Deep discovery**: Interview the owner, key employees, review all systems

• **Knowledge extraction**: Record, transcribe, and catalog every process, every tribal knowledge point, every "only I know how to do this" task

• **System audit**: Map the current tech stack, data flows, integrations, and gaps

• **Baseline metrics**: Document current financial performance, operational efficiency, and growth trajectory

Phase 2: Documentation (Week 3-8)

• **SOP creation**: Every process documented in searchable, structured format in the MIG brain

• **Knowledge graph build**: All business relationships, customer data, vendor data, financial data connected into a queryable system

• **Dashboard deployment**: Real-time KPI dashboard that any buyer or new operator can understand in 30 seconds

• **Workflow automation**: Identify and automate the top 5-10 repetitive processes (invoicing, follow-ups, reporting, scheduling)

Phase 3: Optimization (Week 8-16)

• **Efficiency improvements**: Now that processes are documented, identify and implement optimizations

• **Financial clean-up**: Ensure books are clean, categorized, and tell a clear growth story

• **Remove owner dependencies**: For every process that requires the owner, build a system or SOP that replaces their involvement

• **Prepare data room**: All documentation, contracts, financials, SOPs organized and ready for buyer review

Phase 4: Handoff (Week 16-20)

• **Buyer-ready package**: Comprehensive business dossier that answers every question a buyer would ask

• **AI transition manual**: "How to operate this business" guide powered by the MIG brain — the buyer can ask questions in natural language

• **Operational continuity**: The terminal continues running after sale, providing the new owner with the same intelligence layer

• **Monthly advisory board**: Optional ongoing terminal subscription for the new owner ($3K-$5K/mo)

This is both a retirement transition service and a sale preparation service.
