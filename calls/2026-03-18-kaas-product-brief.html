What Is KaaS?

Knowledge-as-a-Service is the third tier of Meridian's product offering. While Tier 1 (quick wins) and Tier 2 (terminal deployment) serve individual businesses, KaaS is a **horizontal platform play** — selling API access to specialized knowledge bases that Meridian builds and curates over time.

**The analogy Ely used**: "We have a strong ambition to sell knowledge as a service where people would essentially have API access and they would be able to do these things like if they have to process information. There's only so much time that they have to do the research or to search through a massive database."

---

Why It Matters

The Vector Database Limitation

Most AI systems use vector databases for search. The problem:

• **They only return the top 20 results** — If the gold is at result #40, you never see it

• **No relationships** — Results are isolated points, not connected insights

• **No ontology** — No classification system to ensure accuracy

• **No memory** — Each query is independent, no accumulated intelligence

What Meridian's Knowledge Graph Does Differently

• **Relational search** — Queries traverse relationships between nodes, not just similarity scores

• **Ontology-weighted relevance** — Results ranked by structural importance, not just keyword match

• **Sub-millisecond for complex queries** — Architecture designed for speed across massive datasets

• **Self-learning** — The graph gets more accurate and dense over time

• **Cross-domain connections** — A compliance question might surface a supply chain insight that a vector search would never connect

Product Verticals

1. Tax & Compliance Knowledge Base

**Target**: CPA firms, tax preparers, compliance teams

**Content**: IRC (Internal Revenue Code), state tax codes, recent rulings, IRS guidance, case law

**Value**: "Which of my clients are affected by the new Section 174 rules?" — instant answer across 1,000 clients

**Pricing**: $500-$2,000/mo depending on query volume

**Build time**: 4-8 weeks for core tax code ingestion + ontology expansion

2. E-Commerce Intelligence

**Target**: Amazon sellers, D2C brands, e-commerce operators

**Content**: Amazon ToS, advertising benchmarks, category trends, competitor tracking, review sentiment analysis

**Value**: "What's the average advertising cost in my category this month?" / "Which of my competitors launched a new product in the last 30 days?"

**Pricing**: $300-$1,000/mo

**Build time**: 3-6 weeks (significant overlap with existing MIG content)

3. Franchise & Business Operations

**Target**: Franchise brands, multi-location businesses, PE firms

**Content**: FDD analysis (4,000+ public franchise disclosure documents), industry benchmarks, SOP libraries, compliance frameworks

**Value**: "Which franchise brands under 10 units have declining unit economics?" / "What's the average royalty rate for wellness franchises?"

**Pricing**: $1,000-$3,000/mo

**Build time**: 6-10 weeks (FDD scraping + analysis pipeline)

4. Capital Markets & Due Diligence

**Target**: Investment funds, M&A advisors, PE firms

**Content**: Public filings, market data, regulatory changes, jurisdiction-specific compliance

**Value**: "What regulatory changes in Q1 2026 affect our portfolio companies?" / "Compare compliance frameworks across 5 jurisdictions for this deal"

**Pricing**: $2,000-$5,000/mo

**Build time**: Already partially built — Meridian's existing capital markets ontology + MIG content

5. Security & Compliance Monitoring

**Target**: IT teams, MSPs, compliance officers

**Content**: CVE databases, NIST frameworks, threat intelligence, regulatory requirements by industry

**Value**: "Does our infrastructure meet SOC 2 requirements?" / "What vulnerabilities were disclosed this week affecting our stack?"

**Pricing**: $500-$2,000/mo

**Build time**: 4-8 weeks (significant overlap with existing security content)

6. Energy & Commodities (Future)

**Target**: Energy traders, commodity brokers

**Content**: Weather patterns, demand forecasting data, historical pricing, grid capacity data

**Value**: "Predict electricity demand in Dallas for next week based on weather + historical patterns"

**Pricing**: $3,000-$10,000/mo (high value, low volume)

**Build time**: 8-12 weeks (new domain, requires specialized data sources)

Defensibility

This is the long-term moat Ely described on the call:

1. **Time investment**: Each knowledge base takes weeks/months to build, curate, and validate

2. **Ontology**: The classification system that makes searches accurate — can't be reverse-engineered from API responses

3. **Density**: A knowledge graph with 50,000+ nodes and 500,000+ relationships becomes exponentially more valuable

4. **Continuous learning**: Every query, every new document ingested, every correction makes the graph better

5. **Cross-domain connections**: Insights that span multiple verticals (e.g., tax code change → franchise valuation impact) are unique to having all domains in one graph

Go-to-Market

Phase 1: Embedded in Terminal (Now - Month 6)

• KaaS capabilities are part of the terminal subscription

• Clients experience the knowledge graph through the dashboard and Telegram

• No separate API product yet — building demand through usage

Phase 2: API Access for Terminal Clients (Month 6-9)

• Terminal clients who want to integrate knowledge into their own tools get API access

• Priced as an add-on to existing subscription

• Validates demand, pricing, and usage patterns

Phase 3: Standalone KaaS Product (Month 9-12)

• API access available independent of terminal subscription

• Self-service signup, usage-based billing

• Documentation, SDKs, developer portal

• This is where scale economics kick in
