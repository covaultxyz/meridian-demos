The 2-Minute Conversation

**Them**: "What do you guys do?"

**You**: "You know how most businesses have their information scattered everywhere — email, spreadsheets, different software tools, and a lot of it is just in people's heads? We fix that. We build a digital brain for the business that connects everything, remembers everything, and can actually do work — not just answer questions.

Think of it like getting a CEO-level intelligence layer for your business. It can process your company data, research your competitors, draft documents, handle phone calls, and tell you exactly where you're leaving money on the table.

What makes us different from ChatGPT or any of these generic AI tools is two things: it has a real memory — it doesn't forget what you told it last week — and it has an ontology, which is basically like a Dewey Decimal System for all your business data. That means when it searches for an answer, it doesn't just give you the first 20 results like Google. It gives you the *right* answer because it understands the relationships between everything in your business."

**Them**: "How much does it cost?"

**You**: "Depends on what you need. A simple voice AI that handles your phone calls and books appointments starts around $1,500 a month. A full terminal — the whole operating system with dashboards, multiple AI agents, research capabilities — is typically $3,000 to $5,000 a month. For context, that's less than one junior employee, and it works 24/7.

For some engagements we do performance-based: we only get paid when we deliver measurable results. Zero risk for the client."

Objection Handling Guide

| What They Say | What They Mean | What You Say |

|-------------|---------------|-------------|

| "We already use ChatGPT" | AI = chatbot | "ChatGPT forgets everything you told it. It can't access your business data. It can't make phone calls. It can't monitor your operations. We build a system that does all of that — and it remembers." |

| "AI isn't ready yet" | Burned by hype or bad tools | "You're right that most AI products overpromise. We showed a franchise owner our work product and he called it 11 out of 10. We're happy to do a small pilot and let the results speak." |

| "It's too expensive" | $3K/mo feels risky | "It's less than one employee. And we'll do the first engagement performance-based — you only pay when we deliver measurable results." |

| "I don't understand the technology" | Feature overload | "You don't need to. You'll interact with it through your phone — it's like texting a very smart assistant. We handle all the tech." |

| "My team will resist this" | Fear of replacement | "We don't replace your team. We make them 10x more productive. The time they spend on manual tasks — data entry, answering phones, chasing documents — gets freed up so they can do higher-value work." |

| "What about my data security?" | Legitimate concern | "Your data stays in your system. We don't feed it to OpenAI or Google. The brain runs on secure infrastructure that we manage. It's yours — you own all of it." |

| "Can you work with our existing software?" | Integration worry | "Yes. We connect to whatever you're using — QuickBooks, Salesforce, Google Workspace, Slack, your phone system. We augment, we don't replace." |

| "I want to think about it" | Needs a push | "Totally understand. Would it help if we did a free 2-hour assessment of your business? We'll show you exactly where the opportunities are — no commitment." |
