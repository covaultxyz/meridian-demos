LENS — What's Actually There

RPM Capital is a finance firm operating in capital markets — deal origination, fund structuring, compliance, and investor relations. The terminal serves as a persistent intelligence layer for capital markets decision-making: what deals are worth pursuing, what compliance requirements govern them, what the competitive landscape looks like, and what the legal architecture should be.

Unlike Rankona (operational intelligence for Amazon seller agency), RPM Capital's terminal is about *judgment compounding* — every deal analyzed, every compliance question resolved, every fund structure evaluated enriches the graph for the next decision. The domain surface is capital markets, legal compliance, and deal analytics.

What carries over from MERIDIAN core mesh:

• Knowledge graph as persistent memory (7 research domains, 51K ResearchMemory nodes)

• Contradiction detection (regulatory change invalidates a fund structure assumption)

• Corroboration system (multiple sources confirm a market signal → higher confidence)

• Research pipeline (automated sourcing → extraction → claims → graph enrichment)

• Daemon architecture (specialized agents for distinct operational domains)

What's domain-specific:

• Capital markets vocabulary and ontology (fund types, SEC regulations, deal stages)

• Legal compliance infrastructure (18-doc legal cortex already built, unified instruction set)

• Deal flow analytics (pipeline velocity, conversion rates, deal scoring)

• Investor relations intelligence (LP profiles, commitment patterns, communication cadence)

Data Isolation

RPM Capital data stays on the ghost terminal. Period.

• Separate Neo4j instance (seeded from MERIDIAN MIG, then fully independent)

• Separate NATS server (no cross-connect to MERIDIAN mesh)

• Ghost terminal generates its own embeddings locally (stripped from seed)

• Full export capability (Neo4j dump — client owns their data)

• Only connection to core mesh: drydock (controlled, audited, one-directional updates)
