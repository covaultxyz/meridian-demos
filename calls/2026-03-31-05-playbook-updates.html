Playbook Updates

Systemic Fixes — Effective All Calls After March 27, 2026

---

1. Mandatory Identity Verification Before Research Begins

**Problem**: Research profiled the wrong Garrett Taylor. No verification step existed.

**Solution**: Before any prep brief is drafted, the person requesting the brief must confirm three things with the connector:

1. Full name + company + role (exact)

2. How they know this person (shared context)

3. What the connector knows about their current situation

If the brief is being prepared from online research without connector confirmation, it must be labeled **UNVERIFIED** and the team must be told explicitly before the call.

**Effort**: Low — 5 minutes of conversation before starting research.

**Impact**: Critical — prevents 100% wasted prep cycles and misaligned pitches.

---

2. Discovery-First Enforcement (No-Pitch Gate)

**Problem**: Alexander pitched within minutes of Garrett finishing his background. The team had no sense of Garrett's actual pain before explaining the product.

**Solution**: Michael, as call facilitator, holds a discovery gate. No product pitch begins until these four questions have been answered by the prospect:

1. *"What's the biggest operational bottleneck in your business right now?"*

2. *"What does winning look like for you in the next 12 months?"*

3. *"What have you tried that hasn't worked?"*

4. *"If you had a magic wand and could change one thing, what would it be?"* (asked at minute 10, not minute 55)

Michael signals the team when discovery is complete. Alexander does not begin pitching before that signal.

**Effort**: Low — requires agreed pre-call roles, not new skills.

**Impact**: Critical — prevents pitching into mismatch and gives the team real anchors for product explanation.

---

3. Pitch Relevance Gate

**Problem**: Alexander's pitch covered Open Claw, 25 terminals, board meeting audio, and Palantir — none anchored to Garrett's context.

**Solution**: Before introducing any feature, the speaker answers internally: *"Which of this prospect's stated problems does this solve?"* If the answer is "none established yet," the feature is held.

Permitted before discovery is complete:

• Memory and context retention (universally relatable)

• Specialization and role clarity (connects to any business with teams)

• The asymmetry between expert knowledge and delegation

Not permitted before discovery:

• Origin stories and legal history

• Technical architecture (NATS, Neo4j, daemon mesh)

• Scale claims (terminal count, node count, client count)

• Demos requiring context to understand

**Effort**: Low — requires discipline and a pre-call briefing on what topics to hold.

**Impact**: High — cuts pitch length by 40%, increases prospect engagement.

---

4. Fundraising Agenda Block (Mandatory When Applicable)

**Problem**: Raising capital was a stated call objective. It was never mentioned. Product explanation expanded to fill all available time.

**Solution**: If fundraising is an objective, it is scheduled as a hard agenda block — last 15 minutes of the call. Michael owns the transition:

> *"I want to shift gears for the last few minutes — we're structuring our round and I wanted to talk about whether that's interesting to you."*

If the call runs long on product discussion, the product discussion is cut — not the investment block. A detailed product walk can be sent after. The investment conversation cannot be had retroactively.

**Effort**: Low — scheduling, not skill.

**Impact**: High — every call where investment is an objective must have this block or the objective cannot be achieved.

---

5. Hard Close Protocol

**Problem**: Call ended with "Let me ponder it" — no date, no deliverable, no ask.

**Solution**: Every call ends with a confirmed next step using this structure:

> *"Before we let you go — I want to make sure we leave with a clear next step. [Specific ask]. Does [date/time] work for you?"*

Acceptable next steps (all concrete and time-bound):

• 20-minute follow-up on a specific topic, date set on the call

• Send a one-pager, with a scheduled date to discuss it

• Trial or demo invitation with a date to review results

• Introduction to a specific person, with a follow-up date

Unacceptable: "I'll be in touch" / "Let me ponder it" / "We'll figure out next steps."

If the prospect exits without a next step: follow up within 24 hours with a specific, low-friction ask. Do not let 48 hours pass without a concrete ask on the table.

**Effort**: Low — requires a pre-defined close and the discipline to say it.

**Impact**: High — "let me ponder it" is a 90% churn rate. A set date is a 3x higher conversion signal.

---

6. Post-Call Debrief Protocol (First 20 Minutes Are Sacred)

**Problem**: 37 minutes of post-call time was spent on Amazon course login recovery, Anthony Graham background checks, and poker planning.

**Solution**: The first 20 minutes after any prospect call are a structured debrief window. Three outputs are required:

1. **Corrected prospect profile** (5 min) — what we learned, what we got wrong

2. **Retrospective** (5 min) — one change to discovery, one change to pitch, one change to close

3. **Next steps** (10 min) — who does what, by when, in what format

Unrelated tasks are deferred until the debrief is complete. If immediate debrief is not possible, it must be scheduled within 24 hours while memory is intact.

**Effort**: Low — 20 minutes of protected time.

**Impact**: High — learnings from each call compound if captured. Discarded after an hour of unrelated tasks, they're gone.

---

7. Demo Readiness Gate

**Problem**: Meridian Copilot was 75–80% complete and could not be shown. It was referenced on the call without being available.

**Solution**: Do not reference a demo asset unless it passes this checklist:

• [ ] Works on a fresh machine without setup

• [ ] Launches in under 60 seconds

• [ ] Has a 5-minute guided walk prepared

• [ ] Has been tested with real audio/data (not placeholder content)

• [ ] Recovers gracefully if something breaks live

If the demo is not ready: do not mention it. Instead offer: *"I'll send you a 3-minute video of what it does before our next call."*

**Effort**: Medium — requires completing the demo before scheduling calls where it's intended to be shown.

**Impact**: Medium — an unavailable demo creates expectation without delivery. A working demo is a self-closing asset.
