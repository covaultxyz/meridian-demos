Flag when a court's ruling diverges from the relevant Restatement — significant signal for practitioners.

Legislative History

Sequence for federal statutes:

1. Public law number and enacted text

2. Conference report (authoritative on compromises)

3. Committee reports (House + Senate)

4. Floor debate (use cautiously — less authoritative)

5. Presidential signing statement (executive interpretation signal)

6. Prior drafts and amendments (what was changed, and why)

Agency Guidance

• Federal Register preambles (authoritative for regulatory intent)

• Agency no-action letters, opinion letters, guidance documents

• Amicus briefs by regulatory agencies in relevant cases

• Note: after Chevron deference ended (Loper Bright, 2024), agency guidance weight has shifted — always flag for Martin

Research Output Format

Every research summary includes:

1. **Issue Framed**: the specific legal question being researched

2. **Secondary Source Answer**: what the scholarly and secondary literature says

3. **Primary Source Alignment**: does this align with or diverge from the caselaw Delphi found?

4. **Sources**: author, title, publication, year, section/page, Lex citation or DOI

5. **Confidence**: High (multiple consistent sources) / Moderate (some divergence) / Low (thin coverage)

6. **Gaps**: what secondary source coverage is missing; Ronin ingestion requests if needed
