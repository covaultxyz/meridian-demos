Pricing Red Flags to Address

1. The $50/hr to $165/hr Jump

Omar was paying $50/hr. Our Tier 1 is $165/hr — 3.3x higher. The framing needs to be:

• "You were paying $50/hr for a website developer. You're now getting a technology company that builds AI-powered clinical systems, maintains the platform, and provides enterprise-grade security. That's a different product."

• Don't compare hourly rates. Compare total value delivered.

2. Omar's "Full Ownership" Stance

Omar says IP ownership is a "deal maker or breaker." The MSLA gives him ownership of the Application Layer (Client Deliverables) but licenses the Infrastructure Layer. This needs careful framing:

• "You own everything custom-built for your practice — every workflow, every screen, every clinical rule. That's what drives your practice valuation."

• "The underlying platform is licensed to you perpetually — you can use it, modify it, migrate it, bring in other contractors. You just can't resell Meridian's platform as a standalone product."

• "Think of it like your EHR: you own your patient data and your practice setup, but you license DrChrono's software. Same model."

3. The Exclusivity Price

$3,000/month might feel high to Omar initially. Framing:

• "This means we won't build the same system for any competing psychiatric practice in the San Juan metro for 2 years. That's your competitive moat."

• "Without exclusivity, we could take what we learned building your system and build something similar for a competitor across town. With exclusivity, you're the only one."

• "At 20 providers, $3K/month is $150/provider/month — less than a single AI scribe subscription per provider."

4. The Deposit

$74,000 upfront (50% of Year 1 services) is significant. Options:

• Reduce to 25% ($37,000) for the first SOW to build trust

• Structure as milestone-based: $37K at SOW execution, $37K at Project 1 delivery, monthly billing thereafter

• Tie to Omar's hardware investment timeline — he's already spending $60-100K on hardware

Recommended Negotiation Approach

1. **Lead with value, not price.** Show Omar the $1.85M/year value creation at 20 providers before discussing costs.

2. **Start with Project 1 only (Tier 2).** $66,600 total. This is close enough to his $50/hr frame ($55K at $50/hr for 1,100 hours vs $66,600 at our rates for 300 hours) that the conversation is about efficiency, not hourly rate.

3. **Introduce exclusivity as a competitive advantage.** $3K/month is positioned as "buying your competitive moat," not as a fee.

4. **Let Omar self-select into Tier 3 when he's ready to sublicense.** When he starts talking about licensing the scribe to other practices, Tier 3 is the natural upgrade.

5. **Keep Tier 4 buyout as the answer to "I want to own everything."** The $1.2-2M price tag makes Tier 2/3 licensing look extremely reasonable by comparison.
