Meridian MSLA v5 — Simple Breakdown

What's New in v5?

V4 added commercialization guardrails (Tier 3 audit rights, embedded-IP resale limits, exclusivity hardening, buyout exclusions). V5 resolves the final question: **is Omar contributing any IP to this deal?**

The answer is no. Omar is a customer, not a co-developer. He provides requirements and patient data — Meridian builds everything. V5 makes this explicit and adds a clean tier escalation path so Omar pays for what he needs, when he needs it.

---

The Two Big Additions

1. "Omar Is a Customer, Not a Co-Developer" (Section 1.3)

This is the simplest and most important section in v5. It says four things clearly:

**Meridian builds everything.** All the software, code, systems, tools, and deliverables are designed and built by Meridian. Omar doesn't write code, contribute algorithms, or bring his own software tools to the project.

**Omar contributes requirements, not IP.** When Omar says "I need a patient intake form that collects insurance information and books appointments," that's a business requirement — not intellectual property. His description of what he wants doesn't make him a co-owner of what Meridian builds.

**Patient data isn't an IP contribution.** Omar's patient records, practice data, and clinical information stay 100% his property. But giving Meridian access to that data so Meridian can build software doesn't mean Omar co-owns the software. The data is his. The software built using that data is Meridian's (licensed to Omar).

**Meridian doesn't owe Omar money.** Because Omar isn't contributing IP, Meridian doesn't owe him royalties, revenue share, or success fees. The money flows one direction: Omar pays Meridian for services and license rights. If Meridian uses its own technology for other clients, Omar doesn't get a cut.

Why This Matters

Without this section, there's a risk that Omar (or his attorney) could later argue:

• "I provided the clinical workflows, so I co-own the software"

• "My patient data trained the system, so I should share in the revenue"

• "We built this together, so it's a joint venture"

V5 closes all of these arguments on page one. Omar is buying a meal, not opening a restaurant with the chef.

---

2. Tier Escalation — "Pay for What You Need, When You Need It" (Section 3.7)

This is the deal-closing mechanism. Instead of forcing Omar to pick his final tier on day one (and pay for rights he doesn't need yet), v5 lets him start simple and upgrade whenever his business is ready.

#### How It Works

Day One: Tier 1 (Free)

Omar pays for the development work. He gets basic internal-use rights at no extra license fee. He can run the software in his own practice. That's all he needs right now.

When Omar Grows: Tier 2 ($15-30K/year)

Omar opens new offices, hires an IT contractor, or wants to move to a different server. He fills out a one-page Tier Upgrade Addendum, pays the difference, and he's done. No new contract. No renegotiation. No lawyer meetings.

When Omar Wants to Sublicense: Tier 3 ($40-100K/year)

Omar decides he wants to license his AI scribe to other psychiatric practices. He upgrades to Tier 3, which gives him sublicensing rights. He pays the Tier 3 fee minus whatever he already paid for Tier 2.

When Omar Wants to Own the Technology: Tier 4 (Separate Deal)

Omar decides he wants to actually buy and own specific pieces of technology. That's a completely separate negotiation with a separate price tag (minimum $50K per asset, or 3-5x development cost). This doesn't happen through the tier upgrade process — it requires a Buyout Agreement (Exhibit G).

#### The Upgrade Process

It's designed to be dead simple:

1. Omar says "I want to upgrade to Tier 2"

2. Meridian sends a one-page Tier Upgrade Addendum within 10 business days

3. Both sides sign it

4. Omar pays the upgrade fee

5. Done — new rights are active immediately

No new SOW. No renegotiation. No lawyers (unless Omar wants one). The pricing is already defined in Section 3.5.

#### What You Can't Do

• **No downgrades** on active projects. If you upgraded to Tier 2, you can't go back to Tier 1 for that project. (But you can pick Tier 1 for your next project.)

• **No retroactive changes.** The upgrade applies going forward — it doesn't change the terms of work already done under the old tier.

#### Why This Is the Right Approach for Omar

Based on what we know about Omar's business:

| Omar's Stage | What He Needs | Tier | Extra Cost |

|---|---|---|---|

| **Right now** | Run the patient portal and AI scribe in his own practice | Tier 1 | $0 (included) |

| **6-12 months** | Hire a contractor to help maintain it, open NJ office | Tier 2 | ~$20K/year |

| **1-2 years** | License the scribe to other psychiatric practices | Tier 3 | ~$60K/year |

| **Maybe someday** | Sell the practice with full IP ownership | Tier 4 | $1.2M+ (buyout) |

Omar doesn't need to commit to Tier 3 pricing on day one. He starts with Tier 1 (free), proves the software works, grows his practice, and upgrades when the business justifies it.

What v5 Does NOT Change

Everything from v4 is still in place:

• **Meridian owns all technology** — platform, tools, frameworks, Background IP

• **Omar gets Client Deliverables** — custom workflows, patient portal, AI scribe, clinical logic

• **4-tier license system** — Standard / Extended / Enterprise / Buyout

• **Tier 3 has audit rights** — quarterly revenue reporting, annual audit, 5% underpayment trigger

• **Embedded IP is protected** — no extraction, no benchmarking, sublicensees get binary only

• **Exclusivity is available** — field-limited, geography-bounded, separately priced ($2-4K/mo narrow)

• **Exclusivity breach is capped** — 2x fees, no business-destruction claims

• **Buyout has exclusions** — generalized improvements, cross-client tools, training data carved out

• **Assignment triggers 180-day transition** — not 90-day walk-away

• **Critical incident support** — 12 months post-transition, 15 hrs/quarter

• **SOW required before any work** — no implied assignment, no informal work capture

• **90-day warranty** with latent defects, security vulnerabilities, spec failures

• **Continuous access** — source code, build instructions, credentials, dependencies throughout the term

---

The Agreement Is NOT

V5 adds two more items to the "what this agreement is NOT" list:

• **Not a joint venture, partnership, or co-development agreement** — Omar is a customer buying services, not a co-developer contributing IP

• **Not a revenue-sharing arrangement** — Omar pays Meridian; Meridian doesn't owe Omar anything from other clients or operations

---

The Bottom Line (v5 Final)

1. **Meridian builds everything. Omar buys what he needs.** No co-development, no joint ownership, no revenue sharing.

2. **Start simple, upgrade when ready.** Tier 1 is free. Tier 2 when you grow. Tier 3 when you sublicense. Tier 4 if you want to buy.

3. **One-page upgrade form.** No new contract, no renegotiation, no lawyers for routine tier changes.

4. **Requirements aren't IP.** Omar's clinical workflows and practice rules inform the design — they don't create co-ownership.

5. **Patient data is sacred and separate.** Omar's data is always his. Giving Meridian access to build software doesn't make Omar a co-owner of the software.

6. **The money flows one direction.** Omar pays Meridian. Meridian doesn't owe Omar royalties, revenue share, or success fees.

7. **Everything from v4 still applies.** Audit rights, embedded-IP protection, exclusivity, buyout exclusions, 180-day transition, continuous access, 90-day warranty.

8. **The tier system is the menu.** Omar sees what each level of rights costs. He picks what makes sense for his business today. He upgrades when it makes sense tomorrow.
