Discovery Call Questions

Prepared for meeting with Carl Helgesson — Rank On Amazon

24 questions across 6 categories + key hypotheses to validate

Important Context

Carl requested this meeting. Posture shifts from "pitch" to "diagnose." Don't present problems. Ask what he's trying to solve. Lead with curiosity and respect — he built a profitable business, won Tier 1 Platinum Partner status, and landed a publicly traded client (Humble Group) all with minimal digital infrastructure.

Opening

Establish context and rapport

1

"What prompted you to reach out to us now?"

Reveals: The trigger event. Whatever he says first is the real priority. Listen for whether it's competitive pressure, client demand, growth ambition, or a specific pain point.

2

"Can you walk us through how Rank On Amazon, KNAA, amaNordic, and Amazonpodden all connect? We see the ecosystem but want to hear how you think about it."

Reveals: How he sees the flywheel. Whether entities are separate businesses or one integrated operation. Which entity drives revenue vs. lead gen.

3

"What would make this a great use of your time today?"

Reveals: His definition of success for this meeting. Reduces our builder's bias by letting him set the agenda. Also signals respect for his time.

Technology & Operations

Understand their current stack and workflows

4

"What tools does your team use day-to-day for Amazon operations — keyword research, listing creation, PPC management?"

Reveals: Whether they use Helium 10, Jungle Scout, manual methods, or a mix. Establishes the baseline for automation potential.

5

"How are you managing client reporting today? What does a client receive from you on a weekly or monthly basis?"

Reveals: If it's slide decks or PDFs, the dashboard is the wedge. If they already have tooling, understand what they're using.

6

"We noticed you use Asana and Calendly. What's your full project management flow from client onboarding to ongoing operations?"

Reveals: Process maturity. Whether they have systematized ops or if each strategist runs their own workflow. Integration opportunities.

7

"How long does it take your team to produce a 'Perfect Listing' for a new product across multiple marketplaces?"

Reveals: Time-per-deliverable baseline. If it's hours per listing across 7 marketplaces, the AI optimization engine becomes immediately compelling.

8

"Have you explored any AI tools for Amazon operations? What's your take on AI in this space?"

Reveals: Whether the AI threat is on his radar. Whether he's excited, skeptical, or worried. Calibrates how aggressively to position AI solutions.

Content & Marketing

Explore content strategy and lead generation

9

"Amazonpodden clearly has a dedicated audience. Have you ever considered turning those episodes into written content — blog posts, guides, social clips?"

Reveals: Whether he's thought about content repurposing. If yes — what stopped him? If no — this is our opening for the content pipeline pitch.

10

"Where do most of your new clients come from today? Is it the podcast, conferences, referrals, or the website?"

Reveals: The actual client acquisition channel mix. If it's 90% referral/podcast, the website gap might be intentional. If he says "I wish we got more inbound," that's the entry point.

11

"We noticed the website doesn't have a blog or much content beyond the core pages. Is that a deliberate choice, or something that just hasn't gotten attention?"

Reveals: Whether the content gap is strategic or neglect. His answer determines whether we frame the content pipeline as "new initiative" or "we'll handle what you've been meaning to do."

12

"Are you targeting English-speaking markets for new client acquisition, or is the focus primarily Nordic brands?"

Reveals: Market expansion ambitions. If English is a target, the content pipeline (Swedish podcast to English blog) becomes a must-have.

Pain Points

Validate assumptions from our audit

13

"What's your biggest bottleneck to taking on more clients right now?"

Reveals: Whether the constraint is capacity (tech solves), pipeline (marketing solves), talent (hiring solves), or by design (he doesn't want more clients). This single answer determines our proposal direction.

14

"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your operations tomorrow, what would it be?"

Reveals: His top-of-mind pain. May not match what we found in the audit. His answer overrides our assumptions.

15

"When you onboard a new client, what's the most time-consuming part of the process?"

Reveals: Where manual effort concentrates in the 7-phase process. If it's the analysis phase — our AI research tools are the wedge. If it's listing creation — the LLM listing engine.

16

"What are your clients asking for that you can't currently deliver?"

Reveals: Unmet client demand. If clients want real-time dashboards and they can't deliver, that's our opening. This frames our solution as client-demand-driven, not tech-push.

Growth & Strategy

Understand ambition and direction

17

"Where do you see Rank On Amazon in 2-3 years? More clients, bigger clients, new markets, or something else entirely?"

Reveals: Growth vision. Scale ambitions determine solution scope. If he wants to double, it's a different conversation than if he wants to maintain.

18

"The Humble Group partnership sounds significant — 40+ brands is a lot. How are you planning to scale operations to handle that volume?"

Reveals: Whether the Humble Group deal is straining current capacity. If yes — this is the most urgent use case for automation.

19

"Are you seeing new competitors entering the Nordic Amazon agency space — especially any that are leading with AI or technology?"

Reveals: Competitive awareness. If he names specific threats, we learn what he's worried about. If he's not aware, we can share market intelligence.

20

"The KNAA training program has 100+ graduates. How many of them go on to use Rank On Amazon's agency services?"

Reveals: Training-to-agency conversion rate. If it's low, there's a CRM/automation play. If it's high, understand why and how we can amplify it.

Budget & Decision-Making

Understand buying process and constraints

21

"Have you worked with a technology partner or development agency before? What was that experience like?"

Reveals: Prior experience with tech vendors. Past bad experiences create objections we need to address.

22

"If we found a good fit here, what does your decision process look like? Is it just you, or are there other stakeholders?"

Reveals: Decision authority and timeline. Founder-led likely means Carl decides alone. But there may be a COO, CFO, or board for the Swedish AB.

23

"Have you allocated budget for technology or marketing infrastructure this year, or is this more exploratory?"

Reveals: Budget reality. At sub-$5M revenue, large tech investments are significant. If exploratory, we lead with free quick wins to build trust.

24

"Would you be open to starting with a small proof-of-concept — say, turning a couple podcast episodes into optimized English blog posts — so you can evaluate our work before any commitment?"

Reveals: Willingness to trial. This is our close — a low-risk next step that demonstrates capability.
