Handling Objections

1. "We're not focused on our website right now"

Response: "Totally understand. This isn't about the website specifically — it's about taking content you've already created and making it work harder in English-speaking markets. The blog posts live on the site, but the real value is showing up on Google when someone searches 'how to sell on Amazon in Europe.'"

2. "We've tried tech partners before and it didn't go well"

Response: "That's usually because the tech company built what they wanted, not what the agency needed. That's why we start small — pick one episode, run it through the pipeline, and you judge the output. No commitment beyond a single test."

3. "What does this cost?"

Response: "The quick fixes — meta descriptions, broken buttons, copyright update — we'd do for free. For the content pipeline, it depends on scope. But the first episode is on us — we want you to see the output quality before any money conversation."

4. "Translation quality won't be good enough"

Response: "Fair concern. We use AI that understands Amazon terminology specifically. But that's exactly why we offer the first episode free — you review the output and decide if the quality meets your standards."

5. "Too busy with Humble Group partnership"

Response: "That's actually related. If you're scaling to 40+ brands, your team's time is the most constrained resource. The content pipeline runs without your team doing anything — we process the audio, you review the output."

Key Principles

  • Lead with their wins, not problems. Platinum. Humble Group. Case studies.
  • Offer a free sample before asking for anything.
  • Match his pace. Swedish business culture is relationship-first.
  • Be specific. "44 episodes" beats "content strategy improvement."

What NOT to Say

  • Never mention the admin email leak — feels invasive
  • Never cite the EUR 1.06B number — methodology unclear
  • Never say "your website is broken" — frame as "untapped potential"
  • Never mention 85 vs 100 years in outreach
  • Never promise all 8 solutions — right-size to 2 max
