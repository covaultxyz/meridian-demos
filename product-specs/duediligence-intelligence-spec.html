1. VC Return Math Engine

Source: mig_search("VC return math 5x failure reverse engineering") — imp 9

Integration point: Financial Analysis.html + analysis AI Insights - financial.html

INPUT: Fund size, check size, valuation, dilution rounds, target IRR
OUTPUT: Required exit valuation, required ARR, viability assessment

THE MATH:
  $100M fund → $20M fees → $80M investable
  50% fail ($40M → $0)
  37.5% moderate ($30M → $60M at 2x)
  12.5% winners ($10M → ?)

  At 5x winner: IRR = 1.1% (FAILURE — below T-bills)
  At 10x: IRR = 5.4%
  At 25x: IRR = 13.4%
  At 70x: IRR = 25% (TARGET)

  Founder reverse-engineering:
  $2M seed at $8M pre → 20% ownership
  Three rounds at 20% dilution → 10.2%
  Need 70x = $140M return
  At 10.2% → company exits at $1.4B
  At 8x SaaS multiple → need $175M ARR in 5-7 years

Implementation: Calculator widget on Financial Analysis page. Input fields for
fund parameters, outputs viability score with pass/fail on IRR target.

4. PE Performance Metrics

Source: PE Fundamentals transcripts (performance measurement + carried interest modeling)

Integration point: Financial Analysis for PE deals

Metric Formula Purpose
IRR Discount rate where NPV of all cash flows = 0 Standard PE performance measure (captures timing)
TVPI Total Value / Paid-In Capital How much the investment is worth as a multiple
DPI Distributions / Paid-In Capital How much cash has actually been returned
J-Curve Cumulative net cash flows over fund life Visualize when fund breaks even (~year 7)

Carried Interest Model:
- Cumulative: If NAV > committed capital → (NAV - committed) × carry% = cumulative carry
- Yearly: cumulative carry - already paid = this year's carry
- LP distributions begin when NAV exceeds committed capital
- GP gets carry, LPs get pro-rata capital return
