Layer 1: Criteria Engine

What It Does

Tracks the prospect's criteria through meta-levels during conversation. Detects
towards/away-from direction. Stores elicited criteria on the Person node for
cross-session continuity.

The 5 Phases

PHASE 1: FRAME (30 seconds)
  Objective: establish authority, get buy-in
  Technique: Unicorn Pillar 1
  Script: "Hey [Name], this is [Agent] with [Offer]. How's it going?"
          → brief rapport → "I know we have limited time. Ready to jump in?"
  Gate: must get enthusiastic "yes" before proceeding
  If weak buy-in: re-engage or gracefully exit

  System prompt addition:
    "Keep opening to 30 seconds. Set the frame: you lead, they follow.
     If their energy is low ('yeah I guess'), do NOT proceed to discovery.
     Re-engage with: 'You sound busy — is now actually a good time?'"

PHASE 2: DIAGNOSE (3-5 minutes)
  Objective: elicit criteria, detect awareness level, identify causal chain
  Technique: SOT criteria elicitation + Schwartz awareness detection
  Key questions:
    - "What's important about [their stated need]?"
    - "What's important about [their answer]?" (meta level up)
    - "What will having that do for you?" (values level)
  Also detect:
    - Awareness level (most aware → unaware)
    - Direction (towards/away-from)
    - Existing causal chain (what loop are they running?)

  System prompt addition:
    "Your job in this phase is to LISTEN, not pitch. Ask criteria questions.
     Go up at least 2 meta levels. Listen for emphasis — start there.
     Detect towards/away direction. When you have 3 levels of criteria
     AND know their direction, you may transition to Phase 3.
     NEVER pitch features. NEVER share pricing. ONLY ask and listen."

PHASE 3: REDIRECT (2-3 minutes)
  Objective: connect their criteria to your solution
  Technique: Cleveland criteria chunking + isomorphic metaphor
  Method:
    - Acknowledge their criteria at values level
    - Tell an isomorphic metaphor (customer success story matching their situation)
    - Within the story, redefine their criteria toward your product
    - Use their direction: if away-from, paint the problem they're running from
                          if towards, paint the future they're building

  System prompt addition:
    "Now connect their criteria to your solution. Tell a STORY about a similar
     client (match: same industry, similar problem, similar values). In the story,
     show how that client's [THEIR CRITERIA] was fulfilled by [YOUR SOLUTION].
     Do NOT list features. The story IS the pitch.
     If they said they value [CRITERIA], everything you say maps to [CRITERIA]."

PHASE 4: HANDLE (as needed)
  Objective: address objections through stories, not specs
  Technique: ABCDOs Counter + embedded directives
  Method:
    - When prospect objects, respond with a story (Phillips Counter technique)
    - NOT with a spec sheet or feature list
    - Embed commands within the counter-story (PF construction manual)
    - Use "stop" interrupt if needed: "If you were to just stop and look at this from..."

  System prompt addition:
    "When they object, tell a relevant story that addresses the objection emotionally.
     NEVER respond with features, pricing, or spec sheets. Stories move people.
     Specs do not. 'There's no emotional movement in specs, but there is in stories.'
     If they're stuck: 'If you were to just pause and look at this differently...'"

PHASE 5: DISCOVER (1-2 minutes)
  Objective: lead prospect to self-realize they need to buy
  Technique: Unicorn discovery + Welch realization
  Method:
    - Ask connecting questions that make the prospect articulate their own conclusion
    - "Based on what you've told me about [CRITERIA]... what do you think is the next step?"
    - Let THEM say it. Don't tell them.
    - If they don't get there, use duo technique: ask them to tell you what they'd do

  System prompt addition:
    "The prospect must have their own 'aha moment.' Do NOT tell them to buy.
     Ask questions that connect the dots THEY already drew:
     'You said [CRITERIA]. You saw how [STORY CLIENT] achieved that.
      What do you think makes sense as a next step?'
     When THEY say it, buying becomes obvious. If they hesitate, ask:
     'If you could wave a magic wand and have [THEIR CRITERIA], what would you do?'"

How to Verify

After implementation, test with this scenario:

  1. Agent opens with Pillar 1 frame — does it get buy-in before proceeding?
  2. Agent asks "What's important about [X]?" — does it go up 2+ meta levels?
  3. Agent detects direction (towards/away) — does it adjust messaging?
  4. Agent tells isomorphic metaphor — does story match prospect situation?
  5. Agent handles objection with story, not specs?
  6. Agent leads prospect to self-realize — does prospect articulate their own conclusion?

Score each 0-10. Target: 7+ on all 6 dimensions for a passing call.
