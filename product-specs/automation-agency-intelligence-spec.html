Product Definition

A productized AI automation agency that helps businesses automate operations using
AI agents, workflows, and integrations. The product is the AGENCY PLAYBOOK — the
system for building, running, and scaling the agency itself, not just the client deliverables.

Architecture: 5 Pillars of Growth (Sequential)

Source: mig_search("5 pillars agency growth ryan stewart") — imp 9

Pillars are SEQUENTIAL. Skipping ahead guarantees failure.

PILLAR 1: POSITIONING & OFFER DESIGN
  │  Single vertical + single problem + measurable client ROI
  │  Test: "I help [vertical] solve [problem] resulting in [metric]"
  │  Example: "I automate appointment booking for med spas, adding 40+ bookings/month"
  │  
  │  OFFER-MARKET FIT CRITERIA:
  │  1. Industry where businesses actively need the automation
  │  2. The automation result is high-dollar or high-frequency
  │  3. Small number of automations justifies the retainer
  │
  ▼
PILLAR 2: PRODUCTIZATION
  │  Build entire automation template BEFORE client signs
  │  Eliminate custom discovery — it's a checklist, not a project
  │  Junior hires execute templates ($40K) vs senior architects ($150K)
  │  Day 1: client is live with working automation
  │  Competitors: still in "discovery phase" at month 2
  │
  ▼
PILLAR 3: SERVICE MASTERY
  │  Best in world at YOUR vertical's automation needs
  │  Learn the client's BUSINESS (buy courses in their industry)
  │  Every client = control in your experiment (flywheel)
  │  Case studies compound as proof → referrals → more clients
  │
  ▼
PILLAR 4: ACQUISITION (3 channels)
  │  Channel 1: Organic social (free, most neglected)
  │    Post consistently about results, problems, client wins
  │  Channel 2: Outbound sales (requires positioning to target)
  │    "I automate [X] for [vertical]" — not "I do AI stuff"
  │  Channel 3: Paid ads (requires audience specificity)
  │    Only works with clear vertical + offer
  │
  ▼
PILLAR 5: SALES
    Scorecard Analysis → Traffic/Impact Projection → Close
    80% proposal close rate by GATEKEEPING proposals
    Sell projected OUTCOMES, not deliverables
    "30 automated bookings/month = $X revenue" beats "we'll build 5 Zapier flows"

Sales Process: Scorecard Analysis

Source: Ryan Stewart Scorecard Analysis framework

Replace "here are your deliverables" proposals with data-driven narratives:

SCORECARD (5 areas, scored 0-100):

  1. AUTOMATION READINESS
     - How many manual processes exist?
     - What's the time cost of manual work?
     - Which processes are repetitive enough to automate?

  2. LEAD CAPTURE EFFICIENCY
     - How are leads currently captured?
     - What's the response time to new inquiries?
     - How many leads are lost to slow follow-up?

  3. CLIENT COMMUNICATION
     - How are clients currently contacted?
     - What's the follow-up consistency?
     - Are there gaps in the communication flow?

  4. OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY
     - How is onboarding handled?
     - What's the task management system?
     - Where are the bottlenecks?

  5. GROWTH INFRASTRUCTURE
     - Is there a referral system?
     - Is marketing automated or manual?
     - What's the capacity for new clients?

FLOW:
  Scorecard → Impact Projection → "If we automate these 3 areas,
  you save X hours/week and capture Y more leads" → Pricing

Pricing Strategy

Source: Ryan Stewart pricing insights

Tier Retainer What's Included
Starter $2,000-3,000/mo 1-2 core automations + maintenance
Growth $5,000-8,000/mo Full automation stack + monthly optimization
Scale $10,000-12,000/mo Custom automations + AI agent deployment + strategy

Pricing rule: Sell projected outcomes, not deliverables.
"$5K/month for 40 extra automated bookings" beats "$5K/month for 3 Zapier workflows."

Client profitability test: Can ONE automated outcome justify the monthly retainer?
If yes → you have offer-market fit. If no → change vertical or raise outcome value.

The $40-50K/month Plateau

Most agencies stall here. Root cause: ALWAYS Pillar 1 failure.
- Selling to anyone who'll pay → commodity pricing
- No productization → every client is custom → margin destruction
- No vertical mastery → generic case studies → weak proof

Fix: Go back to Pillar 1. Pick ONE vertical. Build ONE template.
Master ONE type of automation. The constraint creates the breakthrough.
