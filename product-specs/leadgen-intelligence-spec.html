Product Definition

An AI-powered lead generation service that automates the entire pipeline from
raw prospect data to personalized outreach at scale. Clients provide their offer
and target market; the system delivers qualified, enriched, personalized outreach
campaigns that book calls.

Client Onboarding: Offer One-Sheeter First

Source: mig_search("offer one-sheeter creation framework") — imp 9

Before any lead gen runs, the client must have their offer dialed in. The onboarding
flow uses the Offer One-Sheeter framework:

Step 1: THREE P's WORKSHOP (30 min)
  - Problem: What specific pain points does your market have?
  - Promise: What's the desired future state? ("Sell the future, not the features")
  - Process: What are your 3-5 logical steps from A to B?

Step 2: DREAM CLIENT IDENTIFICATION
  - Who do you LOVE working with?
  - Who can you get RESULTS for?
  - Who can PAY you?
  → If all three don't overlap, refine before proceeding.

Step 3: OFFER NAMING
  Rule: "Tell them the exact result in the fewest words possible"
  Bad: "Digital Marketing Accelerator Program"
  Good: "30 Leads in 30 Days"

Step 4: REVENUE MATH (backward from target)
  $1M target → 100 sales at $10K → 400 calls at 25% close
  → 500 booked at 80% show → 10,000 leads at 5% opt-in
  → Monthly: 833 leads → daily: 28 leads

Step 5: AWARENESS-LEVEL MESSAGING (per Schwartz)
  Map the offer to each awareness level:
  - Most Aware: "Last 10 spots at $X"
  - Product Aware: "Why [Product] vs [Competitor]"
  - Solution Aware: "The 3 types of [Solution]"
  - Problem Aware: "[Symptom]? Here's why."
  - Unaware: "If you value [Identity], this is for you"

Email Sequence Templates

Cold Sequence (Feldman 7-Step)

Source: mig_search("7-step cold email sequence") — imp 8

Email 1: Social proof + CTA
  "I help [avatar] do [result] in [timeframe]."

Email 2: Same-thread bump
  Short reminder, no new content.

Email 3: Case study / YouTube video
  Plain YouTube link (NOT embedded/hyperlinked).
  Give them something to watch.

Email 4: First lead magnet as gift
  "I have a gift I think you'd appreciate.
   Our customers usually pay $100-200 for this."

Email 5: Follow-up on gift + CTA
  "What'd you think? Pretty cool, huh?
   If you got value, imagine what else we can do."

Email 6: Second lead magnet (better than first)
  Escalate value. Include calendar link.

Email 7: Soft guilt close
  "Did you get a chance to try it?
   Either way, I think it's time to chat."

Rules: Never 3+ emails without providing value. Large audience → stop at 2 emails.
Small/niche audience → expand to 7 with lead magnets.

Nurture Sequence (Post-Interest)

  • 10-15 emails over 30-60 days
  • Value-first, CTA in PS only
  • Goal: get them enjoying your emails → they click the calendar link

Reactivation Sequence (Gone Cold 3-6 months)

  • 3-4 emails over 1 week
  • All value, zero selling
  • Goal: re-excite, NOT close

Subject Line Strategy

Source: Feldman "Customer-Mimic" framework

Write subject lines that mimic what the recipient would receive from their own
customers or internal team. The recipient should think "this could be from a client."

E-commerce example: "[Product name] stock status"
Agency example: "Regarding your [city] rankings"
SaaS example: "Integration question about [their tool]"

Omni-Channel Bridge

  1. Send LinkedIn connection requests (manual or automated)
  2. When accepted, wait 10-15 minutes
  3. Send email: "We just connected on LinkedIn. Would love to chat about [X]."
  4. Feed into email nurture sequence
